# Edges

A graph can also be **weighted**, which means that each edge has a numerical value associated with it. This weight can represent, for example, costs, distances, or capacities.

## Weighted Graph Diagram

The diagram shows a directed graph with 6 nodes (A, B, C, D, E, F) and weighted edges:

- **A → F**: 99
- **B → C**: 13
- **F → C**: 10
- **C → D**: 62
- **E → D**: 87
- **E → C**: (unlabeled edge)

The nodes are arranged with A and B at the top, F and C in the middle, and E and D at the bottom, connected by directed arrows labeled with their respective weights.
