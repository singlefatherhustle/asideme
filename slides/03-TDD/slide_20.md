# Unhappy paths

Unhappy paths, on the other hand, require us to think about how our code will handle errors, **edge cases**, and unexpected input.

An **edge case** is a scenario at the "edge" of expected behavior.

What do we expect `multiply` to do if:

- it only receives one argument? What about 3?
- either of the arguments is not a number?
- one of the arguments is 0?
- either of the arguments is negative?
- either of the arguments is a floating point number?
