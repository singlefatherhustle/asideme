Two-party-consent states — read this before recording

Several U.S. states require every party being recorded to give consent, not just one. As of writing these states include California, Connecticut, Delaware, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Oregon, Pennsylvania, Vermont, and Washington. Recording a class, lecture, or conversation in any of these states without explicit consent from every participant — including the instructor and other students — can be a criminal offense. Other jurisdictions (including the EU and UK) have similar or stricter rules. You are solely responsible for complying with the recording laws of your location and the locations of everyone being recorded. If you're not sure, ask first or don't record.
