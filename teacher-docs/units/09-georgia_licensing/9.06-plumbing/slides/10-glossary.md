# Glossary — Key Plumbing Terms

## Air Gap
Physical separation between potable water outlet and a contaminated surface. Highest backflow protection.

## AAV (Air Admittance Valve)
One-way valve that allows air into a drainage system to prevent siphoning. Closes against positive pressure to keep sewer gas out.

## ABS
Acrylonitrile Butadiene Styrene plastic. Drain-waste-vent pipe material.

## Backflow
Reversal of water flow back into the supply system. Can introduce contamination into potable water.

## Backflow Prevention Device
Equipment that prevents back-siphonage or back-pressure (AVB, PVB, DCVA, RPZ).

## Branch Line
Section of pipe serving multiple fixtures off the main line.

## Building Drain
The drainage pipe that receives waste from all the fixture branches inside the building.

## Building Sewer
Drainage from the building to the public sewer or septic.

## Check Valve
A valve that allows flow in only one direction.

## Cleanout
A removable plug providing access for cleaning the drainage line.

## Cross-Connection
Any actual or potential connection between potable water and a contaminating source.

## CSST
Corrugated Stainless Steel Tubing. Flexible gas piping.

## CW
Cold water.

## DFU (Drainage Fixture Unit)
A unit measuring the drainage demand from a fixture. Sum DFUs to size drainage pipe.

## DWV
Drain, Waste, Vent system. The drainage and venting system of a building.

## Expansion Tank
Small tank installed at water heater to absorb expansion when water is heated in a closed system.

## Fixture Unit (FU)
A unit measuring the water-supply demand from a fixture. Sum FUs to size supply pipe.

## Galvanized
Steel pipe coated with zinc. Older material, prone to internal corrosion. Limited use today.

## HW
Hot water.

## IPC
International Plumbing Code. Adopted as the basis for Georgia plumbing code.

## Journeyman
Plumber qualified to work under a master plumber. Cannot operate independently.

## Lavatory
Bathroom sink.

## Master Plumber Class I
Master license restricted to residential and small commercial (≤ 10,000 sq ft).

## Master Plumber Class II
Master license unrestricted for any size and type of plumbing work.

## OS&Y (Outside Stem and Yoke)
Type of valve where the stem screws in and out of the body. Common on fire system supply.

## PEX
Cross-linked polyethylene plastic. Flexible water supply tubing.

## P-Trap
Standard plumbing trap shaped like a P (sideways). Holds water seal against sewer gas.

## Potable Water
Water suitable for human consumption.

## PRV (Pressure-Reducing Valve)
Reduces incoming water pressure to building. Set typically 50-60 psi.

## PVB (Pressure Vacuum Breaker)
Backflow preventer for low-hazard applications under continuous pressure.

## PVC
Polyvinyl Chloride plastic. Most common drainage pipe material.

## RPZ (Reduced Pressure Zone)
Highest-rated backflow preventer for high-hazard applications.

## Sewer Gas
Methane, hydrogen sulfide, and other gases in sewer systems. Toxic and explosive.

## Slope
Downward inclination of drainage pipe (1/4 in/ft for most residential).

## Soil Stack
Vertical pipe carrying waste from upper floors to building drain.

## Stack Vent
Continuation of soil stack above the highest branch to outdoor atmosphere.

## Trap
Water-holding device that prevents sewer gas from entering the building.

## Trap Arm
Horizontal pipe between trap weir and vent fitting.

## Trap Weir
Top of water surface in a trap; the spill-over point during drainage.

## TPR Valve
Temperature/Pressure Relief valve on water heaters. Releases pressure if hot water expands beyond limits.

## UPC
Uniform Plumbing Code. Different from IPC; used in some western states. Georgia uses IPC.

## Vacuum Breaker
Device that allows air into a supply line to break vacuum and prevent back-siphonage.

## Vent
Pipe that admits air into the drainage system to maintain atmospheric pressure.

## Water Closet
Toilet (the technical term in plumbing code).

## Water Heater
Equipment that heats potable water for distribution.

## Water Service
Pipe from utility main to building.

## Yoke Vent
Vent connecting between soil stack and trap arm in a multi-story drainage system.
