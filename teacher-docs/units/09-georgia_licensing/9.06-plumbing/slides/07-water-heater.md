# Water Heaters

## Types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Tank (storage)** | Most common; holds hot water in insulated tank |
| **Tankless (on-demand)** | Heats water as it flows; no storage |
| **Heat pump** | Uses heat pump cycle; very efficient |
| **Solar** | Heated by solar collectors |
| **Combination** | Tankless + heating system combined |

## Tank water heater sizing

Residential:

```
1-2 people: 30-40 gallon tank
3-4 people: 40-50 gallon tank
5+ people:  50-80 gallon tank
```

Tank size also depends on usage pattern (multiple morning showers vs spaced out).

## Tankless sizing

Tankless rated by GPM (gallons per minute) at temperature rise:

```
Cold ground water: ~50°F
Desired hot:       120°F
Temperature rise:  70°F

Required GPM at this rise:
   Single shower:   1.5 GPM
   Shower + sink:   3.0 GPM
   Multiple bath:   5-8 GPM
```

Tankless capacity is the max flow it can deliver at the temperature rise.

## Temperature controls

Code requires:

```
Maximum tank setpoint: 120°F (anti-scald protection)
Maximum at fixture: 120°F (per recent IPC updates)
```

For commercial applications:

- Storage at 140°F+ (Legionella prevention)
- Mixing valve at fixture to bring to 120°F
- Documented thermostat verification

## Connection requirements

### Cold water inlet

- Cold water shutoff at heater
- Expansion tank required when system has check valve or PRV (most modern installs)
- Dielectric union if connecting copper to galvanized

### Hot water outlet

- Hot water out without separate shutoff (already controlled by cold inlet)

### TPR Valve (Temperature/Pressure Relief)

- Required on every water heater
- Discharge pipe must be:
  - Same size as TPR outlet
  - Terminate within 6 inches of floor (or to a drain)
  - Cannot be capped, threaded, or valved
  - Direction of discharge marked

### Gas water heater specific

- Vent connector sized per IFGC
- Combustion air per IFGC
- Drip pipe at gas line entry

### Electric water heater specific

- 30A 240V dedicated circuit
- Disconnect within sight or locked-out at breaker
- GFCI protection at outdoor installations

## Energy efficiency

| Type | UEF (Uniform Energy Factor) |
|---|---|
| Standard electric tank | ~0.92 |
| High-efficiency electric | ~0.95 |
| Heat pump | 2.5-3.5 |
| Tankless gas | 0.82-0.96 |
| Tankless electric | 0.99 |
| Standard gas tank | 0.55-0.65 |
| High-efficiency gas | 0.80-0.95 |

Heat pump water heaters have the highest UEF and qualify for energy rebates.

## Sediment / scale prevention

Annual maintenance:

```
1. Drain flush (open drain valve, flush 3-5 gallons)
2. Anode rod inspection (replace if heavily corroded)
3. Temperature check (verify 120°F setting)
4. Visual inspection for leaks
5. TPR valve manual test
```

Extends water heater lifespan from 8 years to 15+ years.

## Common water heater errors

- TPR valve discharge improperly terminated
- Missing expansion tank when closed system requires it
- Tankless undersized for actual demand
- No combustion air for gas water heater in confined space
- Wrong electrical disconnect (or missing)
- Sediment buildup causing failure (lack of maintenance)
- Mixing pipe materials creating galvanic corrosion
