# Plumbing License Classes

## Three classes

Georgia separates plumbing contractors into three classes:

| Class | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Journeyman Plumber** | Statewide journeyman work; must work under a master |
| **Master Plumber Class I** | Single-family dwellings, two-family dwellings, commercial ≤ 10,000 sq ft |
| **Master Plumber Class II** | Unrestricted (all sizes and types) |

## Journeyman vs Master

```
Journeyman = qualified to perform plumbing work
Master    = qualified to perform AND oversee/operate a business
```

Journeymen work under a master's supervision. They CANNOT:

- Operate their own plumbing business
- Pull permits in their own name
- Supervise other journeymen
- Bid plumbing work

## Class I Master scope

```
✓ Single-family homes
✓ One-level dwellings designed for ≤ 2 families
✓ Commercial structures ≤ 10,000 sq ft
✗ Larger commercial
✗ Industrial process plumbing
✗ Hospitals, schools (typically larger than 10k sq ft)
```

## Class II Master scope

```
✓ Everything Class I covers
✓ Commercial buildings > 10,000 sq ft
✓ Industrial process plumbing
✓ Hospitals, schools, hotels
✓ High-rise residential
✓ Specialty plumbing (laboratory, medical gas)
```

## Experience requirements

| License | Experience |
|---|---|
| Journeyman | 1 year plumbing experience |
| Master Class I | 5 years plumbing + 2 years as journeyman |
| Master Class II | Class I requirements + commercial/industrial experience |

## Why Georgia restricts who has businesses

The board's policy: "The board does not register plumbing companies, but only licensed Master Plumbers may have a business — not Journeymen."

This means:

- A Class I master can have a plumbing business
- A Class II master can have a plumbing business
- A journeyman cannot start a plumbing business
- A journeyman who wants their own firm must upgrade to master license

## The path

```
Year 0:    Get hired as a plumbing apprentice (no license required)
Year 1:    Become journeyman (1 year experience, pass exam)
Year 3:    Continue journeyman work
Year 5:    Eligible for Class I master (2 years as journeyman + 5 total)
            Pass Class I master exam
            Open plumbing business
Year 7+:  Add commercial experience, upgrade to Class II
```

## Common license-tier errors

- Operating as a plumbing contractor without a master license
- Working as journeyman past 5 years without upgrading
- Class I master taking commercial work over 10,000 sq ft
- Working under a master who isn't actively supervising
- Mismatched license type for the work being performed
