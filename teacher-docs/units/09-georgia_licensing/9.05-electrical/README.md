# Electrical — Georgia Study Guide

**Unit:** Georgia Licensing
**Topic:** Electrical Contractors (Class I and Class II)

---

## License Classes

Georgia's electrical licensing rule divides contractors into **Class I** and **Class II**:

- **Class I** — restricted to **single-phase** installations that do not exceed **200 amperes** at the service drop or service lateral.
- **Class II** — **unrestricted**.

## Qualification Requirements

To qualify, an applicant must:
- Be at least **21**
- Submit the application
- Provide **three references** with at least one **licensed electrical contractor**
- Document at least **four years of primary electrical experience**
- Pass the exam with at least **70**

**Up to one year** of experience may be credited from **secondary experience or education**, with **secondary experience credited at 50 percent**.

## Dependable Conduit-Fill Workflow

1. Identify conductor type and insulation so you know the approximate area per conductor.
2. Count all current-carrying conductors and any equipment grounding conductors that must be included by the fill rule used.
3. Use the relevant conduit-fill tables to determine maximum fill percentage and raceway area.
4. Compare total conductor area to permitted fill.
5. If over fill, go up a raceway size or change conductor/routing strategy.
6. Then separately check **ampacity adjustment**, **box-fill**, and **equipment-termination** requirements.

## Sample Exam-Style Questions

**Q:** What is the scope limit of a Georgia Class I electrical contractor license?
**A:** It is restricted to **single-phase electrical installations** that do not exceed **200 amperes** at the service drop or service lateral.

**Q:** Can an applicant qualify with three years of primary electrical experience and two years of secondary experience?
**A:** **Yes.** The rule states secondary experience is credited at **50 percent**, so three years primary plus two years secondary (= 1 credited year) equals the required four years.

## Real-Life Scenario

**Scenario:** An applicant's background is mostly inspection, estimating, and engineering design — with little direct installation.
**Answer:** Georgia recognizes some of that background as **secondary experience**, but the rule emphasizes that electrical contractors need actual in-the-field competence, and secondary/education credit is **limited**. A candidate built almost entirely on secondary experience is a **likely nonqualifier**.
