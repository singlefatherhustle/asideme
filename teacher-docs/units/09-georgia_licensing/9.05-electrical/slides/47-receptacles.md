# Receptacles (NEC Article 406)

## Common receptacle configurations

NEMA designation tells voltage / amperage / grounding scheme:

| NEMA | Voltage | Amps | Common use |
|---|---|---|---|
| 5-15R | 125 V | 15 A | Standard duplex (residential) |
| 5-20R | 125 V | 20 A | T-slot for 20 A circuits |
| 6-15R | 250 V | 15 A | Small 240 V appliances |
| 6-20R | 250 V | 20 A | Window AC, small commercial |
| 6-30R | 250 V | 30 A | Older dryers |
| 6-50R | 250 V | 50 A | Welders, EV chargers |
| 14-30R | 125/250 V | 30 A | Modern 4-prong dryer |
| 14-50R | 125/250 V | 50 A | Range, EV charger |

## The T-slot 5-20R

A 5-20R has a T-shaped neutral slot that accepts both 5-15P (standard) and 5-20P (20 A) plugs. Use 5-20R when:

- Circuit is 20 A and you want 20 A loads to be plugable
- Required for some commercial receptacle counts on 20 A circuits

## Tamper-resistant — 406.12

Required in all 15 A and 20 A receptacles in dwellings (and many other occupancies):

- Spring-loaded shutters block one-blade insertion
- Both blades must enter simultaneously

Tamper-resistant is mandatory in:
- All dwellings
- Hotel guest rooms
- Childcare facilities
- Hospitals (patient care areas)

## Weather-resistant — 406.9

Outdoor receptacles must be:

- **Weather-resistant** type (WR marking)
- Installed in a weatherproof enclosure
- Use an "in-use" cover (closes around the cord while it's plugged in)

## GFCI replacement for non-grounded outlets

Old two-wire homes have receptacles without an EGC. NEC 406.4(D) permits replacement:

```
Existing: 2-wire outlet (no ground)
   ↓
Replace with: GFCI receptacle, marked "No Equipment Ground"
```

This is the legal path to "ground" an old outlet without rewiring. The GFCI provides shock protection even without a ground wire.

## Wet location — 406.9(B)

Receptacles in wet locations must be in an "in-use" weatherproof enclosure:

- Cover stays closed when receptacle is in use
- Listed for the application
- Self-closing or hinged

This is more restrictive than the older "weatherproof while flap is closed" enclosure.

## Common receptacle errors

- 5-15R on a 20 A single-receptacle circuit (allowed only if multiple receptacles share the circuit)
- Missing tamper-resistant in new construction
- Old "while in use" cover not the in-use type
- Reversing line and load on GFCI receptacles
