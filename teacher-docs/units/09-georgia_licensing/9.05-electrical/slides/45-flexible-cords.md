# Flexible Cords and Cables (NEC Article 400)

## What flexible cord is

Multi-conductor cable with stranded conductors designed for flexible use — connecting portable equipment, lamp cords, extension cords, etc.

## Common cord types

| Type | Use |
|---|---|
| **SJ** | Hard service, junior (300V) |
| **SJO** | Oil-resistant SJ |
| **SO / SOOW** | Hard service, oil-resistant |
| **SVT** | Vacuum cleaner cord |
| **NM** is NOT a cord — it's a cable assembly |

## Permitted uses — 400.10

- Pendants
- Wiring of fixtures
- Connection of portable lamps, portable utilization equipment, or appliances
- Stationary equipment for frequent interchange
- Pre-assembled cord drops
- Hoist or crane connections

## Prohibited uses — 400.12

- As substitute for fixed wiring
- Run through holes in walls, ceilings, floors
- Run through doorways, windows
- Attached to building surfaces (with limited exceptions)
- Concealed behind walls
- Installed in raceways (except specific allowed cases)

## Strain relief — 400.14

Where flexible cord enters equipment, fittings must:

- Reduce stress on conductor terminations
- Prevent the cord from being pulled out
- Be appropriate for the cord type

Connectors include compression clamps, listed cord grips, or molded strain reliefs on appliance cords.

## Ampacity — Table 400.5(A)

Cord ampacity depends on:

- Conductor size (e.g., #14, #12, #10)
- Number of current-carrying conductors
- Insulation type

| Size | 2 CCC | 3 CCC |
|---|---|---|
| #18 | 10 A | 7 A |
| #16 | 13 A | 10 A |
| #14 | 18 A | 15 A |
| #12 | 25 A | 20 A |
| #10 | 30 A | 25 A |

## Extension cord rules

While not "wiring" per se, extension cords on job sites must:

- Be listed for the use (hard service rating)
- Have GFCI protection on construction sites (1926.404)
- Be inspected daily for damage
- Never connect through a doorway without protection
- Never staple, nail, or otherwise damage the jacket

## Common cord errors

- Using SJ cord for permanent wiring (forbidden)
- Running flex cord through walls "temporarily"
- Stapling flex cord to a stud
- Missing strain relief on equipment connections
