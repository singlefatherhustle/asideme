# The Three References Rule

## What the rule says

Every applicant must submit **three references**, and **at least one** must be a currently-licensed Georgia electrical contractor. That sentence is the entire rule, but candidates trip over the specifics.

## Who counts as "licensed electrical contractor"

- Holder of a Georgia Class I or Class II electrical license
- License must be **current** at the time the reference is submitted
- License must be in **good standing** (no active disciplinary action)
- The contractor must have known you in a working capacity

## Who does NOT count for that "licensed" slot

- A licensed Master Plumber, GC, or HVAC contractor — different trade
- An out-of-state licensed electrical contractor (unless they hold a current GA license too)
- A former licensee whose license is now lapsed or revoked
- A relative who happens to hold the license but never worked with you

## The other two references

The remaining two references are flexible. Reasonable choices:

- Previous employers (electrical or otherwise) who can speak to work ethic
- Job superintendents or general contractors who watched you work
- Inspectors who interacted with your installations
- Licensed professionals in related trades (architects, engineers, GCs)
- Long-standing clients (for self-employed applicants)

## Reference letter content checklist

Each letter should answer:

```
1. How long have you known the applicant?
2. In what capacity (employer / coworker / client)?
3. What is your assessment of the applicant's electrical competency?
4. Do you affirm the applicant's character?
5. Contact phone for verification.
```

The board does call references at random. Make sure your references know they may be contacted and have the dates/details fresh.

## Real-world scenario

> Applicant submits three letters, all from licensed Georgia electrical contractors he has worked under. **Result:** application sails through this requirement; the board treats this as the strongest reference profile.

> Applicant submits three letters: one from a licensed GA electrical contractor, one from a job-site superintendent, one from a longtime customer. **Result:** also valid — the rule only requires *at least one* electrical contractor.

> Applicant submits three letters from his cousin who happens to hold a GA electrical license. **Result:** the board will likely deficiency it. References must be from people you have actually worked with in a professional capacity.
