# Class II — Unrestricted Scope

## What "unrestricted" actually covers

Class II is the open license. There is no amperage cap, no phase restriction, no occupancy limit. A Class II contractor can legally install:

- Residential at any service size (200 A, 400 A, 600 A, etc.)
- Three-phase commercial (208 V / 480 V)
- Industrial 480 V services and motor control centers
- High-voltage primary work up to where the utility's authority begins
- Photovoltaic systems of any size
- Backup generators and ATS gear
- Healthcare facilities (Article 517)
- Hazardous (classified) locations (Article 500)

## What "unrestricted" does NOT cover

Class II is unrestricted on **scope**, not on **competency**. The board does not waive code knowledge or safety obligations just because your license has no scope cap. You still:

- Need to know NEC chapters that apply to the work
- Need OSHA training appropriate to the environment
- Need insurance that covers the work category
- Are responsible for plan review, permit, and inspection compliance

## The bid impact

Class II is what general contractors look for when subbing out anything beyond residential. Many commercial GCs will not even take a bid from a Class I contractor on jobs over $500 K because the licensing risk is too high.

## When to upgrade

Common career trigger points to move from Class I → Class II:

- Bidding a tenant fit-out for an office or restaurant (almost always 3-phase)
- Adding generator installations to your service mix
- Working on multifamily mid-rise (services aggregate > 200 A)
- Pursuing industrial maintenance contracts

The exam path doesn't change — both classes take the same exam under current rules. The difference is **documented experience** demonstrating you have worked in the higher-scope environment.
