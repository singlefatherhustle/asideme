# Common Pitfalls — What Trips Up Candidates

## On the exam

### Math errors

- **Forgetting the round-trip factor in single-phase voltage drop** (× 2, not × 1)
- **Using single-phase formula on three-phase** (missing √3)
- **Wrong K constant** (12.9 Cu vs 21.2 Al)
- **Forgetting circular mils unit** (using inches² instead)
- **Skipping the 125% multiplier on continuous loads**

### Code lookup errors

- **Wrong NEC edition** (2020 vs 2023 — DCA adopts 2023 effective Jan 1 2026)
- **Wrong table for the question** (Article 220 has 5+ tables for different load types)
- **Reading 60°C column when terminations are 75°C** (or vice versa)
- **Missing exceptions** that change the rule (e.g., 240.4(B) round-up exception)

### Definition mix-ups

- **Grounded vs grounding** (neutral vs EGC)
- **Approved vs Listed** (AHJ acceptance vs published list)
- **Outlet vs receptacle** (point in wiring vs device)
- **Service drop vs service lateral** (overhead vs underground)

## In the field

### Service work

- **Working hot on a service** — utility-side conductors are always live
- **Sharing neutrals between branches** without using multi-pole breakers
- **Re-using Federal Pacific or Zinsco** panels — known fire hazards
- **No GFCI on outdoor receptacles** when adding a circuit

### Branch circuits

- **Splitting 20A receptacles to share neutral** without 2-pole breaker
- **Long conductor runs at minimum size** ignoring voltage drop
- **Missing AFCI in bedrooms** when modifying existing wiring
- **Stapling NM cable too tight, damaging insulation**

### Boxes and devices

- **Too small a box for the conductor count** + devices
- **Forgetting EGC continuity** when using a metal box
- **Wrong device for the application** (residential 15A in commercial 20A circuit)
- **Reverse polarity** (hot to neutral terminal on receptacle)

## Business and law

### Licensing

- **Working on someone else's license** — the qualifying agent must be active
- **Pulling a permit under another contractor's license** — illegal
- **Letting license lapse** while continuing to work — separate violation

### Insurance / liability

- **No GL insurance** for residential work
- **Coverage gaps** between contractor and homeowner policies
- **Not naming additional insured** when working as a sub

### Contracts

- **Verbal change orders** with no documentation
- **Performance ahead of payment milestones**
- **Vague scope** that lets the GC demand extras for free
- **Missing lien deadlines** in Georgia (very specific timelines)

## On the job site

### Tool safety

- **Wrong-voltage rubber gloves** for the work
- **Damaged tools still in use** (cracked gloves, frayed cords)
- **Improvised testing** ("I'll just touch it to see if it's hot")
- **Working alone on energized work** (always have a partner)

### Documentation

- **Missing as-builts** for buried conduits or hidden runs
- **No nameplate / no labels** on disconnects and panels
- **No arc-flash warning labels** on commercial equipment
- **Missing permit final inspection** before customer occupancy

## The big six (most-cited inspector violations)

1. Box fill exceeded
2. Working clearance violations
3. Missing GFCI in required locations
4. Missing AFCI in dwelling habitable rooms
5. Improper conductor support / staple intervals
6. Damaged or improperly grounded equipment
