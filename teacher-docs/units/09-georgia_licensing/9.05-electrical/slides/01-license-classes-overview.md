# Electrical Contractor License Classes

## Two classes in Georgia

Georgia's Construction Industry Licensing Board (Division 553) splits electrical contractors into exactly two classes — no other classifications, no specialty subcategories. Pick the right one for the work scope.

| Class | Scope cap | Typical work |
|---|---|---|
| **Class I** | Single-phase, ≤ 200 A at service drop or lateral | Residential service changes, small additions, single-family new construction |
| **Class II** | **Unrestricted** | Three-phase, commercial, industrial, high-amperage services, motor controls |

## What "service drop or lateral" means

- **Service drop** — overhead conductors from the utility pole to the building.
- **Service lateral** — underground conductors from a transformer pad or pedestal.

The 200 A cap is measured at the *point of utility delivery*, not at sub-panels or branch circuits downstream. A 400 A service with two 200 A panels still exceeds the Class I scope.

## Picking your class

- If your career path is residential service / small commercial → **Class I** is the entry point.
- If you want to bid commercial new construction, industrial work, or anything with three-phase motors → start the experience clock toward **Class II** directly.

## Real-world signal

Most candidates who plan to own a business eventually upgrade to Class II. The scope cap on Class I prevents bidding mid-size and large commercial — a meaningful revenue constraint.
