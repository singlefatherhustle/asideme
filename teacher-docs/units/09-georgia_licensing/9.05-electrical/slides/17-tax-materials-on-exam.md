# Tax Materials on the Exam

## What's tested

The Business & Law portion of the Georgia electrical contractor exam includes a section on **federal employer tax obligations**. The reference for this section is the IRS **Employer's Tax Guide, Publication 15** (current year).

## Topics likely to appear

| Topic | What you need to know |
|---|---|
| **Federal income tax withholding** | How to compute using IRS tables given employee W-4 |
| **Social Security & Medicare (FICA)** | 6.2% SS + 1.45% Medicare = 7.65% employer match |
| **Self-employment tax** | 15.3% total (12.4% SS + 2.9% Medicare) for sole proprietors |
| **Federal unemployment (FUTA)** | 6.0% on first $7,000 of wages; credit up to 5.4% if state UI is paid |
| **State unemployment (SUTA)** | Georgia rate varies by experience rating |
| **Wage base limits** | Social Security wage base updated annually (~$168k in 2024) |
| **Employee vs independent contractor** | 20-factor test summarized in Pub 15-A |
| **Filing schedules** | 941 quarterly, 940 annually, W-2 / 1099 timing |

## Why this is in the exam

A contractor who can't handle payroll obligations turns into a tax delinquency case fast. The board considers basic tax literacy part of the minimum competency for owning a business.

## Sample question pattern

> An employee earned $52,000 in gross wages this year. What is the employer's total FICA contribution match?

```
SS:     6.2% × $52,000 = $3,224
Medicare: 1.45% × $52,000 = $754
Total employer match: $3,978
```

## Pub 15 navigation tips

Tab these sections:

```
1. How to use the wage-bracket tables
2. Social Security and Medicare percentage rates
3. FUTA rules + credit
4. Common employer FAQs
5. Withholding tables (back of book)
```

## What you don't need to memorize

You don't need to memorize the wage-bracket numbers. You're allowed to bring Pub 15 to the exam and look up the actual tax owed for any wage level. The exam is testing your ability to use the reference, not your memorization of IRS tables.
