# NASCLA Business and Law

## What NASCLA is

NASCLA = National Association of State Contractors Licensing Agencies. It maintains an accredited examination that satisfies the **Business & Law** portion of contractor licensing in many states, including Georgia.

## The exam

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Format | Computer-based, multiple choice |
| Questions | ~50 |
| Time | 120 minutes |
| Passing score | 70 (35 correct) |
| Open-book | Yes |
| Reference | *NASCLA Contractors Guide to Business, Law and Project Management* |

## Topic outline

1. **Licensing requirements** — when a license is required, scope, reciprocity
2. **Estimating and bidding** — markup vs margin, common bid mistakes
3. **Business organization** — sole prop, LLC, S-Corp, C-Corp tradeoffs
4. **Financial management** — basic accounting, AR/AP, cash flow
5. **Tax laws** — federal and Georgia employment taxes
6. **Labor laws** — FLSA, OSHA, ADA, EEO, workers' comp basics
7. **Project management** — scheduling, change orders, RFIs
8. **Lien law** — mechanic's liens, preliminary notice, claim of lien
9. **Contracts** — formation, breach, performance, remedies
10. **Risk management** — insurance types, indemnification, warranty
11. **Environmental and safety** — OSHA, EPA basics

## Why electrical contractors take it

Georgia's contractor licensing requires demonstrating business literacy *in addition to* trade knowledge. The Business & Law portion is shared across multiple trades — the same NASCLA exam works for electrical, HVAC, plumbing, and general contractor candidates.

## Reference book essentials

The NASCLA guide is well-indexed. Tab these sections heavily:

- Lien law chapter (Georgia-specific deadlines)
- Project management forms and templates
- Risk management decision tree
- Contracts formation checklist

## Pass rate signal

NASCLA pass rates hover around 60-65% on the first attempt. The most common cause of failure: candidates assume their trade-side expertise carries over to business law. It doesn't. The reading is heavy and the questions test legal vocabulary directly.

Allocate at least 40 hours of dedicated prep for Business & Law, even if you ace the electrical portion.
