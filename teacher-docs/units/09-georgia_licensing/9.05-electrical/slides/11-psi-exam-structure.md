# PSI Exam Structure

## Where you take it

The Georgia electrical contractor exam is administered by **PSI Services LLC** at testing centers throughout the state (Atlanta, Savannah, Macon, Columbus, Augusta, Albany). Walk-ins are not accepted — appointments must be scheduled through PSI's online portal after the board approves your application.

## The exam at a glance

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Vendor | PSI Services |
| Format | Computer-based, multiple choice |
| Passing score | 70 |
| Question count | ~100 (subject to bulletin updates) |
| Time limit | ~5 hours total |
| Open-book | Yes — approved references only |
| Calculator | Approved scientific calculator allowed |

## What's covered

The exam tests competency in:

1. **NEC (National Electrical Code)** — the bulk of the test
2. **Georgia State Electrical Code** amendments to the NEC
3. **OSHA 29 CFR 1926** safety standards
4. **Electrical theory and calculations**
5. **Business and law** (Georgia-specific)

The Business and Law section is sometimes administered as a separate exam, depending on the year's bulletin. Confirm structure on the current PSI candidate information bulletin.

## The day-of routine

- Arrive 30 minutes early
- Bring two forms of ID (one photo)
- Bring your approved reference materials (NEC, Ugly's, etc.)
- Lock all personal items in PSI lockers
- The proctor inspects your reference books — they cannot contain handwritten notes or loose papers
- Sit, take the practice tutorial, begin

## Reference book preparation

Tab and highlight your code books **before** the exam. The proctor will check that:

- All markings are **printed** (no handwritten notes)
- Tabs are professional / pre-printed tabs
- No loose pages, no separate notes inserted

A well-tabbed NEC saves 30+ seconds per code lookup. Across 100 questions that's a meaningful time advantage.
