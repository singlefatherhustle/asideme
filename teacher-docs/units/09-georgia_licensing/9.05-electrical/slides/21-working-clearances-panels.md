# Working Clearances Around Panels (110.26)

## Egress requirement

For equipment rated **1200 A or more** and over 6 ft wide, there must be **one entrance** to the working space at each end at least 24 in wide and 6-1/2 ft high.

For smaller equipment, one entrance is sufficient if:

- The working space provides a continuous and unobstructed way of exit travel
- OR the equipment depth is twice the required workspace depth

## Personnel doors

Personnel doors in the workspace of equipment rated **800 A or more** must:

- Open in the direction of egress
- Be equipped with listed **panic hardware** OR fire-exit hardware
- Open from inside without keys or tools

## Illumination — 110.26(D)

Working space for installations of **service equipment, switchboards, panelboards, or motor control centers** indoors must have illumination. Lighting controls cannot be:

- Automatic only (must include manual override)
- Located only inside the working space (must be reachable without entering)

## Equipment storage

The working space is for working. It is **not storage**. NEC 110.26(B) prohibits using the working space for storage. This is one of the most-cited code violations during inspection — boxes, ladders, conduit racks accumulate in front of panels over time.

## Common inspector callouts

- Stored materials in the workspace
- Suspended ceiling installed too low (< 6.5 ft)
- Door swinging the wrong way (toward the live parts) on 800 A+ rooms
- No manual lighting override
