# OSHA 1926 Subpart K — Electrical

## Scope

OSHA 29 CFR Part 1926, Subpart K covers **electrical safety for construction**. Sections 1926.400 through 1926.449.

## Five major sections

| Section | Topic |
|---|---|
| **1926.400-405** | Installation safety requirements |
| **1926.416-417** | Safety-related work practices |
| **1926.431-432** | Maintenance + environmental considerations |
| **1926.441** | Safety-related work practices (batteries) |
| **1926.449** | Definitions |

## Key 1926.404 — Wiring Design and Protection

Construction site wiring:

- **GFCI protection** required on all 15 A, 20 A, and 30 A receptacles used by personnel
- OR an **Assured Equipment Grounding Conductor Program** (AEGCP) — a written program of periodic ground testing
- Most contractors use GFCI; AEGCP is rare and paperwork-heavy

```
GFCI on every temp receptacle  ← standard practice
OR
Documented inspection + test program  ← rare
```

## Temporary wiring rules

- Cords must be hard-service rated (S, ST, SO, etc.)
- Cords cannot be subjected to physical damage (not run through doorways without protection)
- Cords cannot be stapled or fastened to anything
- Damaged cords must be tagged out and removed from service

## Energized work — 1926.416

Workers must:

- De-energize circuits before work whenever feasible
- Use lockout/tagout to prevent re-energization
- Only qualified persons work on energized circuits, with appropriate PPE

## Approach distances — 1926.417 (similar to NFPA 70E)

Approach boundaries to exposed live parts:

| Voltage range | Limited approach | Restricted approach | Prohibited approach |
|---|---|---|---|
| 50-300 V | 3.5 ft | Avoid contact | Avoid contact |
| 301-750 V | 3.5 ft | 1 ft | 1 in |
| 751 V - 15 kV | 5 ft | 2 ft 2 in | 7 in |

Beyond limited approach, only qualified persons may proceed. At restricted, PPE is required. At prohibited, contact treatment.

## Common Subpart K violations

- Missing GFCI on temp receptacles
- Damaged extension cords still in use
- Workers in approach boundary without PPE
- Live-work without LOTO procedure
- Missing or expired arc-flash labels on switchgear
