# Special Occupancies — Chapter 5 Overview

## What "special occupancies" means

NEC Chapter 5 covers installations where the electrical environment, occupancy use, or risk profile demands rules beyond Chapters 1–4. The most common articles tested:

| Article | Topic |
|---|---|
| **500-503** | Hazardous (classified) locations — Class I, II, III |
| **505** | Class I, Zone 0, 1, 2 |
| **517** | Health care facilities |
| **518** | Assembly occupancies |
| **520** | Theaters, audience areas |
| **525** | Carnivals, circuses, fairs |
| **547** | Agricultural buildings |
| **550** | Mobile homes |
| **551** | Recreational vehicles |
| **555** | Marinas, boatyards |
| **590** | Temporary installations |

## Hazardous (classified) locations — 500

Three classes of hazard:

| Class | Hazard type |
|---|---|
| **Class I** | Flammable gases or vapors (gasoline, propane, paint booth) |
| **Class II** | Combustible dust (grain elevator, sawmill) |
| **Class III** | Easily-ignitable fibers (textile mill) |

Each class has Divisions 1 and 2:

- **Division 1**: hazardous concentration likely under normal operation
- **Division 2**: hazardous concentration only under abnormal conditions

Equipment in hazardous locations must be listed and marked for the specific Class/Division/Group.

## Health care — 517

Hospitals, clinics, surgery centers:

- **Patient care space** definitions (Basic, General, Critical)
- Receptacle and panel requirements scale with care level
- Isolated power systems for operating rooms
- Emergency / essential electrical systems (Type 1, Type 2)

## Pools and fountains — 680

Specific to swimming pools, spas, fountains:

- Equipotential bonding grid around the pool
- GFCI required for nearly all receptacles within 20 ft
- Underwater luminaires must be specifically listed for the use
- Junction boxes for underwater fixtures must be mounted 4 in above max water level

## Temporary installations — 590

Construction site wiring:

- GFCI on all 15 A, 20 A, 30 A receptacles
- Receptacles must be in weather-rated boxes
- Permitted to use SOOW cable for temp branch circuits
- Time-limited: must be removed when permanent power is energized

## Exam strategy on Chapter 5

The full chapter is huge. Focus exam-prep on:

- Hazardous location Class / Division definitions
- Health care patient-care space definitions
- Pool grounding / bonding
- Temporary GFCI rule
