# Ugly's Electrical References

## What it is

Ugly's Electrical References (Jones & Bartlett Learning) is a small, pocket-sized field reference. It's been an electrician's pocketbook for decades, and it's on the Georgia exam's approved reference list.

## What's in it

Ugly's packs the most-used data into ~200 pages:

- **Conduit fill tables** (Chapter 9 NEC tables, reformatted)
- **Voltage drop tables** for copper and aluminum
- **Motor full-load amps** (single-phase, three-phase, DC)
- **Overcurrent device sizing** rules
- **Conductor ampacities** at common temperature ratings
- **Box fill calculations** with worked examples
- **Wire bending and minimum bending space** dimensions
- **Wiring diagrams** for common motor controls
- **Common formulas** (Ohm's, power, three-phase math)
- **NEC tap rules** summarized
- **Math review** (right triangles, square roots, percent)

## Why it's exam-critical

The NEC's tables are spread across hundreds of pages. Ugly's puts the most-referenced data on facing pages with clear headers. For a timed exam, fast lookup beats encyclopedic depth.

**Common exam scenarios where Ugly's wins:**

- "What's the full-load current of a 5 HP three-phase 230 V motor?" → Ugly's motor FLA tables, one flip
- "Maximum number of 12 AWG THHN in 3/4-inch EMT?" → Ugly's conduit fill, one flip
- "Voltage drop on 100 ft of #10 at 20 A, 240 V?" → Ugly's drop tables, one flip

## Tabbing strategy

Most candidates put two big tabs in Ugly's:

```
1. Conduit fill / box fill section
2. Motor FLA tables
```

The rest of the book is small enough to flip-scan within a few seconds.

## Limits

Ugly's is **derivative** — it summarizes NEC tables and rules into pocket form. For ambiguous questions or rule interpretations, go to the NEC itself. Don't argue with the NEC by citing Ugly's; Ugly's is the cheat sheet, not the authority.

## Why every working electrician owns one

Outside the exam, Ugly's lives in tool bags. On a job site you don't pull out the full NEC for a voltage-drop check — you flip Ugly's. The exam rewards using the reference the way it's actually used in the field.
