# Primary Electrical Experience

## The 4-year requirement

To qualify for either Class I or Class II, an applicant must document **four years of primary electrical experience** under the direct supervision of a licensed electrical contractor.

```
   4 full years   minus   ≤ 1 year credited from secondary/education
       ▲
       │
       └── Hands-on installation, maintenance, troubleshooting
```

## What counts as primary

Work performed under the direct supervision of a licensed Georgia electrical contractor (or out-of-state equivalent the board accepts):

- Service installations and upgrades
- Branch circuit wiring (rough-in and trim)
- Panel installation and termination
- Motor connections and motor controls
- Service troubleshooting and repair
- Generator installation and connection
- Photovoltaic system installation
- Code-compliant rework

The work must be **electrical** work — not just being on the job site doing carpentry or helping the electrician.

## What does NOT count as primary

- Hobbyist or homeowner electrical work
- Time on the job site doing other trades
- Time spent supervising others without doing the work yourself (that's secondary)
- Engineering design (secondary)
- Inspecting completed work (secondary)
- Sales or estimating roles (secondary)
- Time in school

## Documenting it

The board wants:

- **Employer name, address, phone**
- **Dates of employment** (month/year start, month/year end)
- **Job titles** held
- **Description of duties** — actual electrical tasks performed
- **License number of supervising contractor**

Vague entries get deficiency'd. "Helped electrician with various tasks" is not enough. "Pulled wire, terminated panels, installed receptacles and switches in residential and light-commercial settings under master electrician supervision" is enough.

## Gaps in employment

Gaps are fine if you can explain them. Document:

- Time you were laid off and what you did during that period
- Time in another trade (it doesn't count toward electrical experience)
- Time in school (may count as secondary depending on program)
- Military service (often counts if MOS was electrical)

## The continuity test

The board does not require continuous employment. Four years of cumulative primary experience over a 6- or 7-year career is acceptable as long as the individual periods are documented.
