# Fixed Electric Space Heating (NEC Article 424)

## Applies to

Fixed electric space-heating equipment installed for comfort heating. Common types:

- Electric baseboard heaters
- Wall heaters / ceiling heaters
- Radiant ceiling cable / mats
- Forced-air electric furnaces
- Hydronic systems with electric boilers

## Branch circuits — 424.3

Branch circuits supplying fixed electric space heating are considered **continuous load** (run more than 3 hours).

```
Conductor and OCPD sizing:
   Required ampacity ≥ 125% × heating load
```

A 5,000 W baseboard at 240 V:
```
Load: 5,000 / 240 = 20.8 A
Required: 20.8 × 1.25 = 26 A
Use 30 A breaker, #10 conductor
```

## Disconnecting means — 424.19

Disconnecting means for fixed heating equipment must:

- Open all ungrounded conductors
- Be within sight from the heater controller
- OR the breaker can serve as disconnect IF it can be locked open

Within "sight" is defined as visible and not more than 50 feet distant.

## Thermostat requirements — 424.20

- Thermostats can serve as the controller but not as the only disconnect
- Line-voltage thermostats (240 V) must be rated for the connected load
- Low-voltage thermostats are separate; they control a relay or contactor

## Heating cables — 424 Part V

Embedded heating cables (in concrete slab, in plaster ceiling, or under flooring):

- Listed for the embedment material
- Mark on each cable at intervals showing voltage, wattage, manufacturer
- Spacing not closer than 1 in between cables
- Cannot be embedded in walls with combustible insulation

## Snow-melt and de-icing — 426

Outdoor snow-melt cables and de-icing cables for gutters / drains:

- Listed for the use
- GFCI protection required at the supply
- Ground-fault equipment-protection (GFEP) at higher ampacities (30 mA trip)

## Common heating errors

- Forgetting the 125% multiplier on continuous heating load
- Disconnect not within sight of the heater
- Using a line-voltage thermostat rated for less than the heater load
- Installing embedded cable in walls that have insulation around it (heat trap)
