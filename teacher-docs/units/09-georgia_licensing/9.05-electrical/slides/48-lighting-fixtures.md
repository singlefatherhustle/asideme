# Lighting Fixtures (NEC Article 410)

## Listing requirement

All luminaires (light fixtures) must be **listed** by a qualified testing laboratory (UL, ETL, etc.). Field-assembled fixtures with non-listed components are not permitted.

## Boxes and supports

Fixtures up to 50 lbs may be supported by an outlet box if the box is:

- Listed for the weight
- Mechanically secured to the building structure

Fixtures over 50 lbs require **support independent of the box**.

```
Ceiling fan support box:
   - Listed for fan support
   - Marked "For Use With Ceiling Fans"
   - Specific weight rating
```

## Clearances — 410.16

For recessed and surface-mounted luminaires:

- **Type IC (Insulation Contact)** fixtures: may be in direct contact with insulation, sealed against air leakage
- **Type Non-IC** fixtures: must be ≥ 3 inches from insulation; ≥ 1/2 inch from combustibles

Older recessed cans in insulated ceilings often violate the 3" rule and are fire hazards.

## Conductor temperature ratings

Fixture wiring within and connected to the fixture must be rated for the temperature inside the fixture:

```
Fluorescent / LED ballast: typically 90°C or higher
Incandescent: up to 200°C or higher
```

Use the conductor type marked on the fixture (often labeled "Use only with conductors rated X°C minimum").

## Track lighting — 410 Part XV

Track lighting:

- Mounted track sections each protected by branch-circuit OCPD
- Heads must be listed for the track
- Total connected lighting load not to exceed listed track capacity

## Outdoor lighting — 410 Part X

Outdoor lighting:

- In wet location requires wet-location-rated luminaires
- Pole-mounted luminaires must have access for splicing inside the pole base
- Pole connections in wet location require sealing fittings or boxes

## Wet location vs damp location

```
DAMP: protected from rain but exposed to humidity (covered porch, garage)
WET: directly exposed to rain or splash (uncovered exterior, near pool)
```

Damp-rated fixtures are permitted in damp; wet-rated fixtures are required in wet.

## Common lighting errors

- Using a non-rated outlet box to support a heavy chandelier
- Insulating around a non-IC recessed can
- Installing a damp-location fixture in a wet location
- Forgetting to use 90°C conductors in fluorescent fixture supply
