# Conductor Identification (NEC 200, 210, 215)

## Grounded conductor — 200.6

Required identification for the grounded (neutral) conductor:

- **#6 AWG or smaller**: continuous white or gray outer finish, OR three white stripes on other color (insulation type permitting)
- **Larger than #6**: white or gray outer finish, OR re-identification at terminations with white tape/paint/markings

## Equipment grounding conductor — 250.119

Required identification for the EGC:

- Continuous green color, OR
- Green w/ one or more yellow stripes, OR
- Bare conductor

## Ungrounded conductors — 210.5(C)

When premises contain branch circuits supplied from more than one nominal voltage system, the ungrounded conductors of each system must be identified:

- By color, marking, or labeling at all termination, connection, and splice points
- A means of identification must be permanently posted at each branch-circuit panelboard

Example: a building with both 120/208 V and 277/480 V panels must have a clear identification scheme so an electrician opening a junction box knows which system the conductors come from.

## Feeder identification — 215.12(C)

Same rule as 210.5(C) but for feeders. When more than one nominal voltage system exists, feeder ungrounded conductors must be identifiable at all access points.

## Switch loops

In a switch loop (sometimes called a "spur"), the white conductor used as the ungrounded "hot" must be re-identified at the switch end:

- Re-identify with permanent marking (paint, tape, sleeve)
- Cannot just leave it white

The 2023 NEC has tightened this — fewer scenarios allow the white-as-hot practice; most installs now require a neutral at the switch for smart-switch compatibility.

## Common identification mistakes

- Using green tape to re-identify a black wire as EGC (not allowed — must be from manufacturer)
- Using white tape to re-identify a black wire as neutral when wire is #10 or smaller (not allowed)
- Failing to post the multi-system identification scheme at the panelboard
- Reusing factory white for switch loops without re-identifying
