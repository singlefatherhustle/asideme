# Cable Tray (NEC Article 392)

## What it is

Cable tray is a structural raceway system for installing and supporting cables in commercial and industrial settings. Multiple cables run side-by-side on the tray, supported continuously.

## Common types

| Type | Description |
|---|---|
| **Ladder tray** | Two side rails with rungs (ladder shape) |
| **Trough / channel** | Solid bottom, ventilated sides |
| **Solid-bottom** | Fully enclosed bottom |
| **Wire mesh / basket** | Welded wire grid, mostly seen in data center applications |
| **Single-rail (center-spine)** | Cables clipped to a single I-beam-style rail |

## Allowed conductors / cables — 392.10

Permitted in cable tray:

- MC, MI, AC cables
- Type TC tray cable (specifically listed for tray)
- Single conductors (1/0 AWG or larger; specific listed types)
- Communications, data, fiber cables
- Optical fiber

## Not allowed — 392.12

- Romex (NM cable) is **not** permitted in cable tray for general use; specific exceptions in 392.10(B)(3)
- Cables not listed for tray use
- Conductors in conduit are not "in tray" — the conduit is in the tray, conductors are inside it

## Cable supports and spacing — 392.20

For single conductors:

- 1/0 through 4/0: lay flat side-by-side
- 250 kcmil and larger: maintain spacing equal to conductor diameter to prevent heating

## Bond and ground

Cable tray itself must be grounded:

- Mechanical connections per 250.92
- For tray runs intended to be part of the equipment grounding system, sized per 392.60 based on largest OCPD upstream

## Fill — 392.22

For multiconductor cables 4/0 or smaller:

- Single-layer fill: cables side-by-side, no stacking
- Fill area limited per the table; typically 50% of tray cross-section

For larger cables, single-layer with maintained spacing.

## Common cable tray applications

- Data center floor distribution
- Industrial plant power and control
- Mechanical room overhead cable bundles
- Large commercial server / telecom rooms

## Common cable tray errors

- Using NM (Romex) in tray — not allowed
- Over-filling without observing the 50% rule
- Forgetting to bond/ground the tray sections together
- Mixing power and signaling cables without separation per 392.20(C)
