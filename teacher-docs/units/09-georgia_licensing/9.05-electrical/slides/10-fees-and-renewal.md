# Fees and Renewal

## Initial fees

| Fee | Amount | Paid to |
|---|---|---|
| PSI exam fee | ~$98 | PSI Services (testing vendor) |
| Application fee | $30 | Georgia Construction Industry Licensing Board |
| License issuance (after passing) | $75 | The board |
| **Total to get licensed** | **~$203** | |

*Fees are subject to legislative adjustment. Always verify current amounts on the GA Secretary of State / SOS Professional Licensing page before mailing.*

## Renewal cycle

Georgia electrical contractor licenses **renew biennially** — every two years. The renewal cycle is set by the board and is the same for every electrical contractor in the state (not by your birthday or anniversary date).

```
   Renewal year ──── Sep 30  →  Oct 1 next year
                         ▲
                Online renewal opens
```

## Renewal fee

- **$75 per renewal cycle** (currently, subject to change)
- Late renewal: full late fee plus reinstatement paperwork
- License expired > 2 years: may require re-examination

## Continuing education (CE) requirement

Georgia electrical contractors must complete:

- **8 hours of continuing education** per two-year renewal cycle
- Courses must be approved by the board
- Must include a portion specifically on the current National Electrical Code
- Records are subject to audit

## Where CE hours come from

Common providers:

- Mike Holt Enterprises (online + in-person)
- IBEW / IEC chapter classes
- Trade-school continuing-education courses
- State-approved private providers

Always confirm the provider's GA approval before paying. Hours from a non-approved provider do not count.

## What happens at renewal

The standard renewal process:

1. Receive notice from the board (~60 days before deadline)
2. Log in to the online renewal portal
3. Affirm CE completion (you may upload certificates)
4. Pay the renewal fee
5. License extends two more years

## Lapsed-license consequences

- **0-30 days late**: pay normal fee plus a small late fee
- **31 days – 2 years late**: full reinstatement paperwork + late fees
- **> 2 years late**: full re-application, may require re-examination, and any work performed while lapsed is unlicensed work — separate civil penalty

Operating with a lapsed license is treated as practicing without a license. The board can fine and refer to AG for criminal charges in extreme cases.
