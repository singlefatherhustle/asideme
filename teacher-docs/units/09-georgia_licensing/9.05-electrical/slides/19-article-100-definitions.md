# NEC Article 100 — Key Definitions

## Why this article matters

Article 100 is the dictionary of the NEC. Every other article assumes you know these definitions exactly as written. Exam questions test definitions verbatim — "approximately" is not enough.

## High-frequency definitions

| Term | Definition (paraphrased) |
|---|---|
| **Ampacity** | Maximum current a conductor can carry continuously without exceeding its temperature rating |
| **Approved** | Acceptable to the authority having jurisdiction (AHJ) |
| **Bonded (Bonding)** | Connected to establish electrical continuity and conductivity |
| **Branch Circuit** | Circuit conductors between the final OCPD and the outlet(s) |
| **Continuous Load** | A load where maximum current is expected for **3 hours or more** |
| **Demand Factor** | Ratio of maximum demand to total connected load |
| **Disconnecting Means** | Device or means by which conductors are disconnected from their source |
| **Feeder** | Conductors between the service equipment and the final branch circuit OCPD |
| **Ground** | The earth |
| **Grounded** | Connected to earth or to a conductive body that extends the ground connection |
| **Grounded Conductor** | Conductor intentionally grounded (the *neutral* in most systems) |
| **Grounding Conductor** | Conductor used to connect equipment or grounded system to a grounding electrode |
| **Identified** | Recognizable as suitable for the specific purpose |
| **Labeled** | Has the symbol of a qualified testing laboratory |
| **Listed** | Equipment, materials, or services included in a list published by a QTL |
| **Outlet** | Point on the wiring system at which current is taken to supply utilization equipment |
| **Overcurrent** | Any current in excess of rated current of equipment or ampacity of conductor |
| **Premises Wiring** | Interior and exterior wiring on the premises |
| **Receptacle** | Contact device installed at the outlet for connection of an attachment plug |
| **Service** | Conductors and equipment for delivering electric energy from utility to premises wiring |
| **Service Drop** | Overhead utility conductors from pole to building |
| **Service Lateral** | Underground utility conductors from transformer to building |
| **Voltage to Ground** | Highest voltage between a hot conductor and ground |

## Three definitions candidates always confuse

```
GROUNDED  vs  GROUNDING
  ↓             ↓
neutral       EGC (equipment grounding conductor)
white         green or bare
```

```
APPROVED  vs  LISTED  vs  LABELED
  ↓           ↓           ↓
AHJ-OK     on a QTL    has the QTL
            list        label affixed
```

```
SERVICE DROP  vs  SERVICE LATERAL
   overhead       underground
```

## Test-day tactic

When a definition-based question feels ambiguous, flip to Article 100 first. The exam author drew the question from the literal NEC wording. Match your answer to those exact words.
