# Glossary — Key Terms

## AFCI
Arc-Fault Circuit Interrupter. Detects the unique current signature of an arcing fault (broken or loose wire) and trips. Required in habitable rooms of dwellings.

## Ampacity
Maximum current a conductor can continuously carry without exceeding its temperature rating. Depends on conductor size, insulation type, and ambient conditions.

## Approach Boundary
Distance from exposed live parts beyond which a person needs PPE or qualifications. Limited, restricted, and prohibited boundaries.

## Approved
Acceptable to the Authority Having Jurisdiction (AHJ). Does not necessarily mean "listed."

## Bonding
Connecting conductive items to establish electrical continuity. Different from grounding (which establishes a low-impedance path to earth).

## Branch Circuit
Circuit conductors between the final overcurrent device and the outlet(s). Branch circuits supply power directly to loads.

## CO/ALR
Marking on a device indicating it's rated for both copper and aluminum branch-circuit wiring. Required when using aluminum branch conductors.

## Continuous Load
Load where maximum current is expected for three or more hours. Requires 125% sizing of conductors and OCPDs.

## CT (Current Transformer)
Reduces large current to a measurable level for metering or relaying. Used on services > 200 A.

## Demand Factor
Ratio of maximum demand to total connected load. Less than 1.0; recognizes that not all loads operate at maximum simultaneously.

## Disconnect
Device that disconnects ungrounded conductors from their supply. Must be within sight of motors, ranges, water heaters, etc.

## EGC (Equipment Grounding Conductor)
Conductor providing a low-impedance path back to source for fault current. Green or bare. Different from GEC.

## EMT (Electrical Metallic Tubing)
Thin-wall steel raceway, joined with set-screw or compression couplings. Article 358 of NEC.

## Feeder
Conductors between service equipment and the final branch-circuit OCPD. Carries the combined load of branches.

## FLA (Full Load Amps)
Motor current at nameplate rated horsepower and voltage. NEC tables provide design values; nameplate provides actual.

## GEC (Grounding Electrode Conductor)
Conductor connecting the grounded neutral to the grounding electrode system. Sized per Table 250.66.

## GFCI
Ground-Fault Circuit Interrupter. Detects current leakage to ground (e.g., through a person) and trips in milliseconds.

## Ground
The earth. Or, by extension, a connection to a conductor that extends the ground reference.

## Grounded Conductor
Conductor intentionally grounded; the neutral in most systems. Color white or gray.

## Grounding Conductor
Conductor used to connect equipment or grounded system to a grounding electrode. The EGC.

## Identified
Recognizable as suitable for a specific purpose. May not be listed.

## IMC (Intermediate Metal Conduit)
Steel raceway, thinner than RMC but threaded. Article 342.

## Labeled
Carries the symbol of a qualified testing laboratory.

## Listed
Equipment or material included in a list published by a qualified testing laboratory.

## LOTO
Lockout/Tagout. Procedure to prevent unexpected energization during maintenance. OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147.

## MWBC (Multiwire Branch Circuit)
Branch circuit with two or more ungrounded conductors sharing a common neutral. Requires multi-pole breakers.

## NEC
National Electrical Code. NFPA 70. The foundational electrical safety standard adopted by states.

## NEMA
National Electrical Manufacturers Association. Their numbering system identifies receptacle and plug configurations.

## NM (Nonmetallic Sheathed Cable)
Romex. Plastic-jacketed cable for residential branch circuits. Article 334.

## OCPD
Overcurrent Protective Device. Fuses or circuit breakers.

## Outlet
Point on the wiring system at which current is taken to supply utilization equipment. May be a receptacle, a light fixture, an appliance, etc.

## PF (Power Factor)
Ratio of true power (kW) to apparent power (kVA). Indicates how efficiently AC current is converted to useful work.

## PPE
Personal Protective Equipment. For electrical: AR clothing, rubber gloves, eye/face protection.

## Qualified Person
Per NFPA 70E: trained and skilled in the construction and operation of the equipment, knows hazards involved, can identify live parts.

## RMC (Rigid Metal Conduit)
Threaded steel raceway. Heaviest of the rigid types. Article 344.

## Service
Conductors and equipment delivering electric energy from the utility to the premises wiring.

## Service Drop
Overhead utility conductors from utility pole to building.

## Service Lateral
Underground utility conductors from a transformer to a building.

## SF (Service Factor)
Motor nameplate factor indicating how much overload the motor can handle without overheating. Common: 1.0, 1.15, 1.25.

## SWA / SWD
Switch-rated; switching duty. Markings on switches indicating they're rated for the duty.

## Tap
Conductor of smaller ampacity branched from a larger conductor, where the OCPD is upstream and the tap is short. 10-foot, 25-foot tap rules.

## UFER
Concrete-encased electrode. Rebar or copper wire embedded in the concrete footing, used as a grounding electrode.

## VD (Voltage Drop)
Voltage loss along a conductor due to its resistance. NEC recommends ≤ 3% branch, ≤ 5% total.

## VFD
Variable Frequency Drive. Electronic motor controller that varies output frequency to change motor speed.
