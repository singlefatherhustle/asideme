# EMT, IMC, and RMC

## The three rigid raceways

Three metal raceways are commonly used to enclose conductors. From lightest to heaviest:

| Raceway | Wall thickness | NEC article |
|---|---|---|
| **EMT** (Electrical Metallic Tubing) | Thin-wall | 358 |
| **IMC** (Intermediate Metal Conduit) | Medium-wall | 342 |
| **RMC** (Rigid Metal Conduit) | Thick-wall | 344 |

## EMT — the workhorse

- Most common in commercial construction
- Joined with set-screw or compression couplings (NO threading)
- Bent with a hand bender or hydraulic bender
- Lighter, cheaper, faster install
- Cannot be used in concrete in some seismic zones
- Not allowed in hazardous (classified) locations

Typical use: exposed runs in offices, retail, warehouses; concealed in non-bearing walls.

## IMC — the middle ground

- Thinner than RMC but threaded ends
- Lighter to handle than RMC
- Can be used everywhere RMC can be used (most cases)
- More expensive than EMT but more robust

Typical use: outdoor exposed runs, areas with elevated impact risk.

## RMC — the heavy duty

- Thickest, strongest, most expensive
- Threaded couplings (must be cut and threaded on the job site or pre-threaded)
- Permitted in hazardous (classified) locations
- Required in some severe-duty environments
- Heavy: a 10-ft length of 2" RMC weighs ~50 lbs

Typical use: industrial, hazardous locations, primary service runs, direct-burial in some configurations.

## Allowable bends — chapter 9 table 2

Minimum radius of bend (for raceways 3/4" and larger) varies by size. Examples for 3/4" trade size:

| Raceway | Min radius (1 shot) | Min radius (field) |
|---|---|---|
| EMT 3/4" | 5" | 4.5" |
| IMC 3/4" | 6" | 4.5" |
| RMC 3/4" | 6" | 4.5" |

Tight bends cause conductors to scrape against the inside, damaging insulation.

## Maximum bends per run — 358.26, 342.26, 344.26

Maximum of **360° of bends** total between pull points. Common run configuration:

```
Box A ──[90°]──[90°]──[90°]──[90°] = 360° total → next pull box required
```

Plan box placement so total bends don't exceed 360°.

## Fill — Chapter 9 Tables

- 1 conductor: 53% fill allowed
- 2 conductors: 31% fill allowed
- **3 or more conductors: 40% fill allowed**

Use Table 5 (conductor properties) + Table 4 (raceway area) to compute exact fill.

## Common raceway errors

- Mixing fittings (EMT compression on IMC threaded)
- Too many bends without a pull box
- Over-filling with too many conductors
- Using a strap with wrong-size groove for the raceway diameter
