# AC Cable (BX)

## What it is

Armored cable, commonly called by the trade name **BX**. Conductors inside a flexible aluminum or steel armor with an internal bonding strip. Governed by NEC **Article 320**.

## How it differs from NM (Romex)

| Feature | NM Cable (Romex) | AC Cable (BX) |
|---|---|---|
| Outer covering | Plastic sheath | Flexible metal armor |
| EGC | Separate green wire | The armor itself + bonding strip |
| Physical protection | Low | Higher |
| Allowed in commercial | Limited | Yes |
| Use in exposed runs | Restricted | More permissive |
| Cost | Cheaper | More expensive |

## Allowed uses — 320.10

- Branch circuits, feeders, services
- Dry locations
- Concealed or exposed work
- Embedded in plaster (older buildings)
- Through air voids in masonry not subject to dampness
- Multifamily and commercial allowed

## Prohibited uses — 320.12

- Wet locations
- Storage battery rooms
- Hoistways (except specific approved types)
- Hazardous locations (Class I, II, III)
- Embedded in poured concrete

## EGC handling

The aluminum or steel armor PLUS the internal bonding strip together form the equipment grounding conductor. There is no separate green wire. The internal strip ensures continuity even if the armor's spiral wrap loses connection.

At each termination, the bonding strip must be folded back over the armor at least 2 inches to ensure the connector bites the bonding strip and creates a positive ground path.

## Bend radius — 320.24

Minimum bend radius: **5 × the diameter** of the cable. Tight bends crack the armor and break continuity.

## Securing — 320.30

- Within 12 inches of every box, cabinet, or fitting
- Then every 4-1/2 feet along the run
- Where flexibility is needed (e.g., last 24 inches to a fixture), the support intervals may be longer

## Anti-short bushings

Always install red plastic anti-short bushings ("redheads") at every cut end of AC cable. These prevent the cut edge of the armor from chafing the conductor insulation. Inspectors look for the red plastic showing in the connector.

## Common BX errors

- Forgetting the anti-short bushing
- Sharp bend that breaks the armor
- Not folding the bonding strip back
- Using BX in a wet location
