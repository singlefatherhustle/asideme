# NEC Edition Changeover (2020 → 2023)

## The Georgia timeline

| Date range | NEC edition in force |
|---|---|
| Through Dec 31, 2025 | **2020 NEC** + Georgia amendments |
| **Jan 1, 2026 onward** | **2023 NEC** + Georgia amendments |

The Department of Community Affairs (DCA) handles statewide adoption. PSI exam reference lists are updated when DCA adopts a new edition.

## Why two editions matter on the exam

If you sit the exam in 2025 → study the **2020 NEC**.
If you sit in 2026 or later → study the **2023 NEC**.

The two editions are 90% identical but the changes are concentrated in high-frequency exam topics:

## What's different in 2023

| Area | Key 2023 changes |
|---|---|
| **GFCI** (210.8) | Expanded to more locations; new equipment categories |
| **AFCI** (210.12) | Broader coverage; reduced exceptions |
| **Receptacles** (210.52) | Tamper-resistant scope expanded |
| **Energy storage** (Article 706) | Major restructuring, new sizing rules |
| **EV chargers** (625) | New listed-equipment provisions |
| **Surge protection** (230.67) | Now required at services for dwellings |
| **PV systems** (690) | New rapid shutdown rules |

## Practical study tip

Buy the edition matching your exam date. Old copies on used-book sites are tempting but can mislead on the exam if the edition shifted.

## Verifying your edition

The cover and spine of every NEC clearly state the year. The 2020 NEC has a brown spine; the 2023 NEC has a blue spine. Check before you walk into PSI.
