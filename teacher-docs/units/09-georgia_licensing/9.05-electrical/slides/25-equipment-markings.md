# Equipment Markings and Labeling

## Required markings — NEC 110.21

Every piece of electrical equipment shall be marked with:

| Marking | Where required |
|---|---|
| **Manufacturer's name / trademark** | All listed equipment |
| **Voltage rating** | Switchgear, panels, motors |
| **Current rating** | Breakers, fuses, panels |
| **Wattage / horsepower** | Motors, heating equipment |
| **Listing label** | Equipment requiring listing under NEC |
| **Short-circuit current rating (SCCR)** | Industrial control panels |
| **Field-applied label** for arc flash, modifications | When required by AHJ |

## Listing labels

Common listing labs and their marks:

```
UL    — Underwriters Laboratories
ETL   — Intertek
CSA   — Canadian Standards Association
NSF   — NSF International (some equipment)
```

Listed equipment carries the testing lab's mark plus a control number traceable back to the listing.

## Field markings — 110.16 (arc flash warning)

Switchboards, panelboards, industrial control panels, meter socket enclosures, and motor control centers in non-dwelling occupancies shall be **field-marked** with an arc-flash warning label visible to qualified persons before examination, adjustment, servicing, or maintenance.

The label must indicate:

- Nominal system voltage
- Arc flash boundary
- At least ONE of: available incident energy (cal/cm²) at working distance, OR site-specific PPE category from NFPA 70E

## Available fault current — 110.24

Service equipment in other than dwelling units shall be legibly field-marked with:

- Maximum available fault current
- Date of calculation
- Update if modifications affect the calculation

This is a 2011 NEC addition that frequently shows up as a deficiency on commissioning.

## "Listed" vs "Approved" — exam trick

| Word | Meaning |
|---|---|
| **Listed** | On a published list by a qualified testing lab (UL, ETL, etc.) |
| **Labeled** | Has the lab's symbol affixed |
| **Approved** | Acceptable to the AHJ (might or might not be listed) |
| **Identified** | Recognizable as suitable for the purpose |

A piece of equipment can be approved by the AHJ even without being listed — the AHJ has authority over local installations. But "listed" is a specific, testable criterion.

## When equipment markings save you

If an inspector challenges an installation, the equipment label is your first line of defense. Markings prove:

- Equipment is appropriate for the voltage/current
- Equipment is listed for the use
- Modifications are documented in field labels
