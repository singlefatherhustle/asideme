# Switches (NEC Article 404)

## Switch types

| Switch | Purpose | Markings |
|---|---|---|
| **Single-pole (SP)** | One on, one off, one location | "1 PI" |
| **Three-way** | Same load, two locations | "3 WAY" |
| **Four-way** | Same load, three or more locations (with two 3-ways) | "4 WAY" |
| **Double-pole (DP)** | Switches two ungrounded conductors simultaneously | "2 PI" |
| **Snap action** | General-use; spring-driven contacts | most common |

## Ratings — 404.14

Common standard ratings:

| Voltage | Common amperage |
|---|---|
| 120 V | 15 A, 20 A |
| 277 V | 15 A, 20 A |
| 480 V | up to 30 A or higher (industrial) |

The switch's voltage and amperage rating must equal or exceed the circuit it controls.

## Switching of grounded conductors — 404.2

The grounded (neutral) conductor shall NOT be switched unless:

- The switch simultaneously opens all ungrounded conductors (in some cases)
- A pole arrangement specifically opens grounded and ungrounded together

In dwellings, **the neutral may not be switched alone** (you can't switch off just the neutral while leaving the hot live).

## Three-way and four-way wiring

```
Three-way switching:

Source ──[3W A] ─traveler─[3W B]── Load

Two travelers carry the alternating path between switches.

Four-way switching:

Source ──[3W A] ─traveler─[4W] ─traveler─[3W B]── Load

4-way switch in the middle, two 3-ways at the ends.
```

## Where switches must control loads — 404.4

Switches shall be located so the controlled load is visible from the switch OR the means of disconnection shall be capable of being locked open.

A workshop ceiling fan controlled by a switch in another room without indication of operation is a violation if the fan isn't visible.

## Neutral conductor at switch box — 404.2(C)

The 2014 NEC and beyond requires a neutral conductor at MOST switch locations in dwelling units. This is for compatibility with smart switches, LED dimmers, and other devices that draw a small standby current.

Exceptions:

- Switch enclosures with metal raceway access to a neutral
- Snap switches that don't need a neutral (specific listings)

## Common switch errors

- Using a 15 A switch on a 20 A circuit (must be 20 A switch)
- Switching the neutral instead of the hot
- Three-way wired with hot to traveler instead of common
- No neutral at the switch box in new dwelling construction
