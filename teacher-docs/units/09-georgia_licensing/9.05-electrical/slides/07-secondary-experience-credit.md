# Secondary Experience — The 50% Credit Rule

## The headline

Some non-installation electrical work counts toward your 4 years — but only at **half value** and capped at **1 year total credited**.

```
2 years of secondary experience = 1 year of primary-equivalent credit
3 years of secondary experience = STILL only 1 year credited (cap)
```

## Activities that qualify as secondary

The board generally accepts these as secondary electrical experience:

| Activity | Credit |
|---|---|
| Electrical engineering / design work | 50% |
| Electrical inspection | 50% |
| Electrical estimating | 50% |
| Electrical drafting (CAD) | 50% |
| Electrical instruction / teaching | 50% |
| Electrical project management (supervisory) | 50% |
| Power-systems analysis | 50% |
| Formal electrical education (post-secondary) | 50%, capped |

## The math

To reach the required 4 years using a mix:

```
3 years primary + 2 years secondary
   3.0  +  (2.0 × 0.5 = 1.0)
   ─────────────────────────────
              = 4.0 ✓ qualifies
```

```
2 years primary + 4 years secondary
   2.0  +  (4.0 × 0.5 = 2.0  →  capped at 1.0)
   ─────────────────────────────
              = 3.0 ✗ does NOT qualify
```

The cap is the trap. Stacking lots of secondary will not substitute for primary beyond the first year of credit.

## Why this rule exists

Georgia recognizes that electrical engineers and inspectors have meaningful electrical knowledge — but the board's mission is to certify *contractors who can actually install code-compliant work*. A candidate built entirely on design and inspection has the theory but may lack the field competency that homeowners and inspectors rely on.

The 50% credit lets engineering / inspection contribute, but ensures hands-on time is the bulk of the qualification.

## Documenting secondary experience

For each block of secondary time, document:

- The role title and employer
- The specific electrical functions performed
- How those functions related to code-compliant installation work
- Continuing education hours / credit hours earned (for formal schooling)

Engineering experience in particular needs to clearly tie to electrical systems — not "general engineering at a power company."

## Real-world scenario

> An applicant has spent his career as an electrical inspector for a municipality (4 years) and never installed work himself.
>
> Math: 0 primary + 4 secondary = 0 + 1.0 (capped) = **1 year total credited**.
>
> Verdict: **Does not qualify.** Needs 3 more years of primary experience before applying.
