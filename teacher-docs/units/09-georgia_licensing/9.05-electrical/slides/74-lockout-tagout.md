# Lockout/Tagout (LOTO)

## What LOTO is

A procedure to protect workers from the unexpected energization of equipment during service or maintenance. Mandated by OSHA 29 CFR 1910.147 (general industry) and applied to electrical work via 1926.

## The eight LOTO steps

```
1. Prepare for shutdown — identify all energy sources
2. Notify affected personnel
3. Shut down the equipment using normal stopping procedure
4. Isolate energy sources (disconnect, valve closure, mechanical block)
5. Apply lockout / tagout devices (locks and tags)
6. Release / restrain stored energy (springs, capacitors, hydraulics)
7. Verify isolation (test for absence of voltage)
8. Perform work
```

To restore:

```
9. Inspect work area, replace guards
10. Notify personnel
11. Remove locks/tags (each authorized person removes their own)
12. Re-energize and verify
```

## Lockout device requirements

- Substantial (not a flimsy lock)
- Identifies the person who installed it (name or ID)
- Cannot be removed without bolt cutters or destructive force
- Each authorized worker applies their own lock (not a single group lock)

## Group lockout

For complex equipment with multiple workers:

- A "lockout box" or group lockout device holds the keys to all energy isolations
- Each worker places their personal lock on the box
- Equipment can only be re-energized when all personal locks are removed

This prevents one worker from being trapped in an energized hazard while a coworker assumes the equipment is locked.

## Tags

Tags supplement locks but never replace them where lockout is possible:

```
DO NOT START / DO NOT OPERATE
By: [Worker name]
Date: [Date applied]
```

Tag must:

- Be visible at the disconnect
- Identify the person who applied it
- Be capable of withstanding the environment (outdoor, wet, etc.)

## Tagout-only situations

A few cases where lockout isn't physically possible:

- Equipment with no lockable disconnect
- Some older equipment retrofitted with new procedures

OSHA requires written justification for tagout-only situations and additional protective measures.

## LOTO and electrical work

For electrical equipment:

1. Open the disconnect
2. Apply your personal lock
3. **Test for voltage with a calibrated meter** at the point you'll be working — both phase-to-phase AND phase-to-ground
4. Apply temporary protective grounds where appropriate
5. Begin work

Failure to test for voltage is the #1 cause of electrical fatalities during maintenance work.

## Common LOTO violations

- Using shared/master locks (each worker needs their own)
- Skipping the voltage test
- Tagout-only when lockout is feasible
- Removing someone else's lock (criminal in most jurisdictions)
- Restoring power without notifying personnel
