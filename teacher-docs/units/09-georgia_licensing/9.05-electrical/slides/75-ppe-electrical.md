# PPE for Electrical Work

## Why PPE matters

When working on or near energized circuits, PPE prevents:

- **Electric shock** from contact with live parts
- **Arc flash burn** from radiated heat
- **Arc blast** from pressure wave + projectiles

The PPE category is determined by the **incident energy** at the working distance — the energy a worker would absorb in a worst-case arc flash event.

## NFPA 70E PPE categories

| Category | Min arc rating | Typical PPE |
|---|---|---|
| **1** | 4 cal/cm² | Arc-rated long sleeve, pants, safety glasses |
| **2** | 8 cal/cm² | Arc-rated coverall or jacket+pants, balaclava |
| **3** | 25 cal/cm² | Arc-flash suit, hood, gloves |
| **4** | 40 cal/cm² | Heavy arc-flash suit, hood, gloves |

## Required components

| Body part | PPE |
|---|---|
| **Eyes** | Safety glasses always; arc-flash hood for Cat 3+ |
| **Face** | Arc-rated face shield (Cat 1) or hood (Cat 2+) |
| **Hands** | Class 0 (1000V) or higher rubber insulating gloves + leather protectors |
| **Head** | Hard hat (Class E rated for electrical) |
| **Body** | Arc-rated clothing matching the cal/cm² rating |
| **Feet** | EH-rated boots (no metal exposed) |

## Rubber gloves

Voltage-rated rubber gloves by class:

| Class | Max use voltage |
|---|---|
| Class 00 | 500 V AC |
| Class 0 | 1,000 V AC |
| Class 1 | 7,500 V AC |
| Class 2 | 17,000 V AC |
| Class 3 | 26,500 V AC |
| Class 4 | 36,000 V AC |

Rubber gloves must be:

- Visually inspected before each use
- Air-tested before each use (inflate and check for leaks)
- Electrically tested every 6 months
- Worn with leather protectors over them

## Clothing rules

Arc-rated (AR) clothing:

- Self-extinguishes when ignited
- Provides specific cal/cm² rating
- Cannot be polyester or any synthetic that melts
- Must cover all exposed skin

Common AR materials: Nomex IIIA, Indura cotton, treated cotton/nylon blends.

## What you cannot wear

- Synthetic non-AR clothing (polyester, nylon underwear)
- Loose hair (must be tied back)
- Metallic jewelry (rings, watches, necklaces)
- Conductive eyewear frames

## When PPE is required

Anytime you work on or near energized equipment:

- **Above 50 V** — generally requires shock PPE (gloves, eye protection)
- **Arc flash hazard analysis** required before working on equipment ≥ 50 V

For routine work in de-energized state with verified LOTO, full PPE is not required — but glasses and gloves are always smart.
