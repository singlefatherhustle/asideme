## Executive Summary

This unit indexes a state-law study guide for Georgia licensing across **real estate**, **electrical**, **plumbing**, **conditioned air**, and **residential/general contracting**. The most important test insight is that Georgia exam questions tend to live at the intersection of **law, rule, and field practice** — not just "what the statute says," but **what a broker, contractor, or qualifying agent must do next** when money, advertising, disclosures, permits, code books, or supervision are involved.

For Georgia real estate, the core state-law stack is **O.C.G.A. Title 43, Chapter 40** (real estate brokers and salespersons), **O.C.G.A. Title 10, Chapter 6A** (Brokerage Relationships in Real Estate Transactions Act), and **GREC Rules Chapter 520-1**, especially brokerage relationships, trust accounts, advertising, transaction handling, and licensees acting as principals. The exam-ready pattern is to memorize **timing rules**, **required writings**, **trust-account handling**, **advertising disclosures**, **record retention**, and **when written disclosures must be made**.

For the Georgia trades, the main legal split is between **O.C.G.A. Title 43, Chapter 14 + Board Rules Department 121** (electrical, plumbing, conditioned air) versus **O.C.G.A. Title 43, Chapter 41 + Department 553** (residential and general contractors). The biggest practical exam point is that **license scope matters**: Class I vs Class II, journeyman vs master, residential-basic vs light commercial, general contractor vs limited tier — all change what work is permitted, what experience is required, and what exam path you must take.

## Major Exam-Version Warning

Georgia's **current statewide construction codes** were updated effective **January 1, 2026** to 2024 ICC codes for building/plumbing/mechanical/fuel gas and the **2023 NEC**. Several publicly posted trade exam reference lists and the publicly indexed PSI contractor bulletin still point to older code editions or were explicitly effective only through **December 31, 2025**.

**Study the legal structure now, but verify the exact current PSI/reference list before test day.**

## Source Notes

The most current rule books I located:
- **Georgia Real Estate Commission rules** current through April 24, 2026
- **State Construction Industry Licensing Board rules** current through April 24, 2026
- **DCA current-code page** and "green sheet" confirming the statewide mandatory construction-code editions effective **January 1, 2026**

The **contractor PSI bulletin** verified publicly is a **2020** bulletin. It is still useful for exam format, time limits, open-book status, and topic breakdown, but it is **not** a substitute for a current candidate handbook pulled directly from PSI immediately before the exam.

The **electrical, conditioned air, and plumbing reference lists** located publicly are official Georgia SOS documents, but each is expressly limited to exams administered through **December 31, 2025**. Use them as a study framework, not as your final packing list for a 2026 exam day.

For Georgia real estate, GREC explicitly tells candidates to obtain the current candidate handbook from **PSI**. GREC's own materials confirm that approved schools currently offer:
- Salesperson Pre License
- Broker Pre License
- Salesperson Post License (**25 hours**)

GREC materials also state that licensees complete **24 hours of approved CE during each four-year renewal period**.

## Open Questions and Limitations

The biggest unresolved item is **version control**. Current Georgia rules and current DCA code adoptions are verified, but the publicly accessible **contractor PSI bulletin** is older and the official trade reference lists located are explicitly limited through **December 31, 2025**. For a 2026 test date, confirm the latest PSI/reference documents before relying on any exact allowed-book list.

For **Georgia real estate**, current GREC rules and current approved-course listings are verified, but a clearly current, GREC-hosted, publicly indexed PSI real-estate candidate handbook was not located. GREC's own materials direct candidates to PSI for the handbook, so treat the current PSI handbook as the final word on the exact modern exam format and logistics.

Because official Georgia OCGA text is not always publicly indexed in a convenient way, some statute references rely on accessible code mirrors for wording support. Where that happened, the practical explanations are grounded primarily in current Georgia board rules and official board publications.
