# Residential & General Contractor License Classes

## Four classes

Georgia's residential / general contractor structure:

| License | Financial responsibility | GL insurance | Contract cap |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Residential Basic** | $25,000 | $300,000 | No cap |
| **Residential Light Commercial** | $25,000 | $500,000 | No cap |
| **General Contractor** | **$150,000 net worth** | $500,000 | No cap |
| **General Contractor Limited Tier** | $25,000 net worth | $500,000 | **$1,000,000 per contract** |

## Why four classes

The structure recognizes different practice areas:

- **Residential Basic**: single-family homes
- **Residential Light Commercial**: residential + some light commercial work
- **General Contractor**: commercial work, no contract size cap
- **GC Limited Tier**: commercial work but capped per-contract size (for newer firms with lower net worth)

## Exam paths

| License | Exam path |
|---|---|
| Residential Basic | Georgia Residential Basic + Georgia Business & Law |
| Residential Light Commercial | Georgia Residential Light Commercial + Georgia Business & Law |
| General Contractor | **NASCLA Accredited Exam** + Georgia Business & Law |
| GC Limited Tier | NASCLA Accredited Exam + Georgia Business & Law |

The NASCLA exam is a national exam accepted by Georgia (and many other states). It's challenging — 81 correct out of 115 needed.

## Trade exam formats

| Exam | Questions | Time | Pass | Format |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Residential Basic | 80 | 200 min | 70 | Open-book |
| Residential Light Commercial | 90 | 230 min | 70 | Open-book |
| NASCLA Accredited | 115 | 300 min | 81 correct | Open-book per bulletin |
| Georgia Business & Law | 50 | 120 min | 35 correct (70%) | Open-book |

## License upgrade path

```
Residential Basic → Residential Light Commercial → 
   General Contractor Limited Tier → General Contractor
```

Each step:
- More demanding exam
- Higher financial requirements
- Broader scope

## Financial responsibility

The board accepts multiple methods of proof:

1. Surety bond
2. Letter of credit from bank
3. Cash deposit
4. Audited financial statement

For General Contractor's $150,000 net worth requirement, candidates typically provide an audited or reviewed financial statement from a CPA.

## The "Limited Tier" reality

A new contractor with limited capital can:

```
Path A: Start with Residential Basic ($25k responsibility)
        Build experience and capital
        Upgrade to higher class when ready

Path B: Start with GC Limited Tier ($25k responsibility, $1M cap)
        Bid commercial up to $1M
        Build capital for full GC ($150k net worth)
        Upgrade when financially able
```

## Common license-tier errors

- Bidding outside scope (e.g., Residential Basic bidding light commercial)
- GC Limited Tier accepting a contract over $1M
- Letting net worth drop below $150k while holding full GC license
- Missing the Business & Law portion (passed trade but not business law)
- Failing to maintain proof of financial responsibility
