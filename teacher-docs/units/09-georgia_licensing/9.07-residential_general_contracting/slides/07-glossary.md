# Glossary — Residential & General Contracting

## Affidavit
A sworn statement made in writing, often required for licensing documents.

## AIA
American Institute of Architects. Publishes standard contract forms widely used in construction.

## Allowance
Funds set aside in a contract for items not yet fully specified (e.g., flooring "allowance" of $5,000).

## ATR
Authorized Trade Representative. A licensed tradesperson designated to act on behalf of an unlicensed entity.

## Bid Bond
Surety bond guaranteeing the bidder will enter into a contract if selected. Forfeited if bidder backs out.

## Building Code
Established standards for construction (IBC, IRC, IECC, IMC, IFGC, etc.).

## Builder's Risk Insurance
Property insurance covering a building under construction.

## CCD (Construction Change Directive)
Owner-issued directive that requires the contractor to perform additional work pending agreement on cost.

## Change Order
Written agreement modifying the contract scope, schedule, or price.

## Closeout
Final phase of a project including punch list, lien waivers, manuals, certificate of occupancy.

## CO (Certificate of Occupancy)
Issued by AHJ when building meets code and is safe to occupy.

## Critical Path
The longest sequence of dependent tasks; determines the project's minimum duration.

## Cost-Plus Contract
Contractor is reimbursed for actual cost plus a fee. Typical for unclear scopes.

## Final Acceptance
Owner's formal acceptance that the project is complete and meets contract requirements.

## Float (Slack)
Time available on non-critical-path tasks without affecting project completion.

## General Contractor (GC)
Contractor with overall responsibility for project execution, typically holding the prime contract with the owner.

## GMP (Guaranteed Maximum Price)
Cost-plus contract with a ceiling on total cost; overruns absorbed by contractor.

## IBC (International Building Code)
Comprehensive code for commercial building construction.

## IRC (International Residential Code)
Code specific to one- and two-family dwellings.

## Lien
Legal claim against property for unpaid work or materials. See: Mechanic's Lien.

## Lien Waiver
Document by which a contractor releases (or conditionally releases) lien rights upon payment.

## Lump Sum Contract
Fixed-price contract; contractor bears risk of cost overruns.

## Markup
Percentage added to cost to arrive at selling price. Different from margin.

## Margin
Selling price minus cost, expressed as a percentage of selling price.

## Mechanic's Lien
Legal claim for unpaid construction work or materials.

## NASCLA
National Association of State Contractors Licensing Agencies. Maintains the accredited general contractor exam.

## Notice of Commencement
Document filed by owner before construction starts, establishing the project for lien-rights purposes.

## Notice of Furnishings
Document filed by a subcontractor or supplier indicating they're working on the project.

## OCIP / CCIP
Owner-Controlled / Contractor-Controlled Insurance Program. Insurance arrangement covering all parties on a large project.

## Performance Bond
Surety bond guaranteeing the contractor will perform per contract.

## Punch List
List of remaining items to be completed before final acceptance.

## Qualifying Agent
Licensed individual designated as the responsible party for a contracting business.

## RFI (Request for Information)
Formal question from contractor to architect/engineer about ambiguities in drawings.

## Schedule of Values
Itemized listing of contract amount allocated to specific work items for payment purposes.

## Sub (Subcontractor)
A contractor working under a general contractor.

## Substantial Completion
The point at which a project is sufficiently complete that the owner can use it for its intended purpose.

## Surety Bond
Three-party contract where surety guarantees contractor's performance to obligee (owner).

## Time and Materials (T&M)
Contract type where contractor is paid for actual hours worked plus materials at cost.

## Tradesperson
Skilled worker in a specific trade (electrician, plumber, HVAC tech, etc.).

## Warranty
Contractor's promise to fix defects within a specified period after completion (typically 1 year for new construction).

## Work Made for Hire
Construction work performed under contract; the owner owns the result.

## Workman's Compensation
Insurance covering employee injuries on the job; required in Georgia for businesses with 3+ employees.
