# NASCLA Accredited Exam

## What NASCLA is

National Association of State Contractors Licensing Agencies. Maintains an accredited examination accepted by multiple states (including Georgia) for general contractor licensing.

## Exam at a glance

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Vendor | NASCLA (administered by Prometric or PSI in Georgia) |
| Format | Computer-based, multiple choice |
| Total questions | 115 |
| Time | 300 minutes (5 hours) |
| Passing score | 81 correct (70.4%) |
| Open-book | Yes (specific reference manual) |
| Reference | NASCLA Contractors Guide and other specific texts |

## Topic distribution

```
General trade knowledge:                  ~20%
Construction project management:          ~15%
Plan reading + estimating:                ~15%
Building codes (IBC, IRC overview):       ~15%
Safety (OSHA):                            ~10%
Foundations + structural:                 ~8%
Concrete + masonry:                       ~7%
Wood framing:                             ~5%
Sustainability + energy:                  ~5%
```

The exam is broad — it tests general contractor competency, not deep trade specialization.

## Acceptance across states

The NASCLA accredited exam is accepted in many states:

- Georgia
- Alabama  
- South Carolina
- Tennessee
- Louisiana
- Mississippi
- Virginia
- North Carolina
- Texas (with some additions)

This means: pass NASCLA once, qualify for general contractor in many states (with each state's separate license application).

## What gets tested

### Project management
- Critical path method
- Schedule management
- Change orders
- RFIs (Requests for Information)
- Punch lists
- Substantial completion

### Estimating
- Quantity takeoff
- Unit pricing
- Markup vs margin
- Contingency
- Overhead allocation

### Codes
- IBC general principles
- Occupancy classifications
- Construction types
- Fire-resistance ratings
- IRC overview (residential building)

### Safety
- OSHA construction industry (1926)
- Fall protection
- Excavation safety
- Lift safety
- LOTO
- Confined spaces

### Trades overview
- Concrete and reinforcement
- Steel framing
- Wood framing
- Insulation
- Roofing
- Finishes

## Studying for NASCLA

```
Time commitment: 80-120 hours typical
Cost of materials: $400-800 (NASCLA + supplementary)
Practice exams: critical for passing

Recommended:
- NASCLA Contractors Guide
- PSI Practice Tests
- Online review courses
```

## Common NASCLA errors

- Treating it like a trade-specific exam (it's general contractor)
- Skipping the math sections (small section but counts)
- Underestimating safety questions (10% of the exam)
- Not familiar with code books (have to navigate IBC quickly)
- Spending too much time on individual questions

## After passing

NASCLA certification:

- Valid for 10 years
- Renewable with continuing education
- Doesn't expire (but the issuing state's license requirements may change)
- Can be used to apply for license in any accepting state
