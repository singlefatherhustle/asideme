# Georgia Business and Law Exam

## What it is

The Georgia Business and Law exam is required for both residential and general contractor licensing. Same exam for both — they test the same business and legal competency.

## Exam at a glance

| Attribute | Detail |
|---|---|
| Format | Computer-based, multiple choice |
| Questions | 50 |
| Time | 120 minutes |
| Passing score | 70% (35 correct) |
| Open-book | Yes |
| Reference | Georgia-NASCLA Contractors Guide to Business, Law and Project Management |

## Lien law — Georgia specifics

Georgia lien law has specific deadlines:

```
Materialmen / Subcontractor:
   Mechanic's lien must be filed within 90 days of last work date
   Notice to owner: required if no direct contract
   Foreclosure must commence within 1 year

For each project:
   Notice of commencement (recorded by owner)
   Preliminary notice (filed by sub/material supplier)
   Notice of furnishings (within 30 days of first work)
   Lien filing (within 90 days of last work)
   Lawsuit to foreclose (within 1 year)
```

Missing any deadline forfeits the lien right. This is heavily tested.

## Contracts

Key concepts:

```
Formation: offer + acceptance + consideration + capacity + legal purpose
Breach: material vs immaterial; remedies
Performance: substantial completion; punch list
Termination: for cause, for convenience, mutual
```

Common contractor contracts:

- Cost-plus
- Lump sum (fixed price)
- Time and materials
- Cost-plus with GMP (Guaranteed Maximum Price)

## Estimating and bidding

```
Markup vs margin:
   Markup % = (Selling price - Cost) / Cost × 100%
   Margin % = (Selling price - Cost) / Selling price × 100%

Bid math:
   Cost: $40,000
   Markup 30%: $40,000 × 0.30 = $12,000
   Selling price: $52,000
   
   On $52,000 sale with $40,000 cost: margin = ($52k-$40k)/$52k = 23%
```

These are different numbers from the same data. Tested directly.

## Risk management

```
Required insurance types:
- General liability (per license class)
- Workers' compensation (employee count threshold)
- Vehicle (commercial auto for company trucks)
- Equipment / property
- Errors and omissions (some scopes)
- Umbrella (excess over base policies)
```

## Common Business and Law exam errors

- Confusing markup with margin
- Wrong Georgia lien filing deadline
- Federal vs state tax treatment differences
- Missing the FLSA threshold for overtime
- Wrong OSHA reporting requirement for incidents
- Confusing employee vs independent contractor classification

## Strategy

Allocate 40-60 hours of dedicated prep for the Business and Law exam, even if you ace the trade portion. Many candidates trip on:

- Lien law (Georgia-specific deadlines are unforgiving)
- Tax classifications (federal vs state)
- Worker classification (employee vs contractor)
- Project management terminology

The pass rate is typically 60-70% on first attempt.
