# Georgia Energy Code

## What it is

Adopts the 2018 IECC (International Energy Conservation Code) with Georgia amendments. Sets minimum requirements for:

- Building envelope insulation
- Window U-values and SHGC
- Air leakage
- Duct sealing
- Mechanical equipment efficiency
- Lighting efficiency

## Climate zones

Georgia spans multiple climate zones:

- **Zone 2** — southern Georgia (Savannah, Valdosta)
- **Zone 3** — most of Georgia (Atlanta, Augusta, Macon)
- **Zone 4** — north Georgia mountains (rare in residential)

Climate zone determines insulation R-values and equipment efficiency requirements.

## Minimum HVAC equipment efficiency

For residential equipment manufactured for installation in Georgia:

| Equipment | Minimum efficiency |
|---|---|
| Central AC (single-phase) | 14 SEER (2023+ standard) |
| Heat pump (split system) | 14.3 SEER2 / 7.5 HSPF2 |
| Gas furnace | 80% AFUE (south) / 90% AFUE (north) |
| Heat pump water heater | 2.0 UEF |

These are FEDERAL minimums under DOE; Georgia adopts them by reference. Equipment sold for installation must meet these.

## Duct leakage requirements — Section 403

In new construction:

```
Total leakage to outside ≤ 4 CFM per 100 sq ft of conditioned floor area
   at 25 Pa test pressure
```

Tested via duct leakage test (Duct Blaster):

- Test all ducts under construction conditions
- Pressurize to 25 Pa
- Measure airflow needed to maintain pressure
- Calculate CFM/100sqft

Leakage above the threshold means duct sealing is inadequate.

## Building envelope air tightness — Section 402

```
Air leakage ≤ 5 ACH50 (Air Changes per Hour at 50 Pa)
   in Zones 1-2
≤ 3 ACH50
   in Zones 3-8
```

Tested with a blower door:

- Mount blower door in main entry
- Pressurize/depressurize house to 50 Pa
- Measure CFM50
- Convert to ACH50

Common Georgia residential targets: 3-5 ACH50.

## Insulation requirements

For Zone 3 (most of Georgia):

| Component | Minimum R-value |
|---|---|
| Ceiling | R-38 |
| Exterior wall | R-13 (cavity) or R-15 cont. |
| Floor over crawl | R-19 |
| Slab edge | R-5 to 24" depth |
| Basement wall | R-5 |

## Window U-value and SHGC

For Zone 3:

```
U-factor ≤ 0.35
SHGC ≤ 0.25 (Solar Heat Gain Coefficient)
```

Most modern double-pane low-E windows meet this. Single-pane or older aluminum-frame windows don't.

## What the HVAC contractor cares about

- Sized AC/heat pump to Manual J load (oversizing wastes energy)
- Tight ductwork to meet leakage test
- Right equipment efficiency rating for jurisdiction
- Proper commissioning + verification

## Inspector requirements

For permitted residential HVAC work, expect:

- Duct leakage test (often required for new construction)
- Equipment efficiency verification (model numbers checked against AHRI database)
- Commissioning report (proper charge, airflow verified)
- Manual J documentation (often required)

## What changes 2026

Georgia's 2024 IECC adoption:

- Higher insulation requirements
- Lower duct leakage thresholds
- Higher equipment efficiency
- New ventilation requirements
- Carbon monoxide detection in new construction
