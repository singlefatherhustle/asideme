# Common HVAC Pitfalls

## On the exam

### Math errors

- Forgetting to convert nominal capacity to design conditions
- Using wrong refrigerant pressure table (R-22 vs R-410A)
- Confusing SEER and SEER2
- Wrong climate zone for the location
- Mixing CFM and BTU/hr

### Code lookup errors

- Wrong code edition (2018 vs 2024)
- Wrong section of Mechanical Code vs Fuel Gas Code
- Misapplying duct leakage thresholds
- Wrong CFM/person rate for occupancy type
- Combustion air calculation errors

### Procedure errors

- Skipping Manual J in favor of "rule of thumb"
- Wrong superheat/subcooling target for the equipment
- Forgetting to verify static pressure
- Recovery without proper procedure

## In the field

### Sizing

- Oversized equipment causing short-cycling and humidity problems
- Undersized for the actual load (rare; usually due to skipping Manual J)
- Mismatched coil and condenser combinations
- Wrong indoor coil for the climate (high-altitude unit in low-altitude application)

### Ductwork

- Crushed flex duct (kinks reduce airflow dramatically)
- Sharp bends (more than 90° in tight space)
- Undersized returns (causes high static)
- Missing return air in bedrooms (door undercut not enough)
- Improper duct sealing (Mastic, not duct tape)

### Refrigerant handling

- Topping off without diagnosing leaks
- Venting during service (illegal)
- Mixed refrigerants from improper recharge
- Charging without proper procedure
- Wrong gauge set or refrigerant cylinder

### Electrical

- Inadequate breaker for the equipment
- Wrong size conductor for the run length
- Missing disconnect within sight of outdoor unit
- Improper bonding/grounding
- Working hot without proper PPE

### Combustion safety

- Missing combustion air openings
- Improper vent connector sizing
- Mixed metals in flue
- CSST without proper bonding
- Cross-contamination of fresh air and exhaust

## Business and law

### Licensing

- Working over Class I scope cap
- Letting license lapse during ongoing job
- No qualifying broker for the firm
- Subcontracting to unlicensed help on permitted work

### Documentation

- No permit for permit-required work
- Missing commissioning report
- No proof of EPA 608 certification on file
- No proof of insurance current at job start
- Verbal change orders without documentation

### Safety

- No fall protection on rooftop work
- Working hot without proper PPE
- No SDS for chemicals
- Untrained worker handling refrigerant

## The big six (inspector pulls)

1. Equipment oversized (no Manual J on file)
2. Duct leakage exceeded threshold (no leakage test, or failed test)
3. Combustion air inadequate for fuel-burning equipment
4. CSST not bonded
5. No commissioning report
6. Missing efficiency rating verification (AHRI lookup mismatch)

## Pre-handoff checklist

```
☐ Manual J on file for the job
☐ Manual D / static pressure measurement complete
☐ Manual S / equipment selection verified
☐ Refrigerant charged correctly (charge or subcooling/superheat documented)
☐ Airflow verified at registers
☐ TSP measured and within manufacturer specification
☐ Ducts sealed and insulated
☐ Equipment commissioning report complete
☐ Customer walkthrough on operation
☐ Permit final inspection passed
☐ Warranty paperwork transferred to customer
☐ All disconnects labeled
```
