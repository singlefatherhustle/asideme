# Glossary — Key HVAC Terms

## ACCA
Air Conditioning Contractors of America. Publishes Manual J (load calc), Manual D (duct design), Manual S (equipment selection), Manual T (air distribution), etc.

## AFUE
Annual Fuel Utilization Efficiency. Percentage of fuel converted to useful heat over a heating season. Higher = more efficient.

## AHRI
Air-Conditioning, Heating, and Refrigeration Institute. Certifies matched equipment performance.

## Air Handler
Indoor unit that contains the blower and evaporator coil. May or may not include heating elements.

## ASHRAE
American Society of Heating, Refrigerating and Air-Conditioning Engineers. Sets technical standards.

## BTU
British Thermal Unit. Energy to raise 1 lb of water by 1°F. Used for heating/cooling capacity (BTU/hr).

## Capacity
Rated heating or cooling output of equipment, in BTU/hr or tons.

## CFM
Cubic Feet per Minute. Volume of air movement.

## COP
Coefficient of Performance. Heating output divided by electrical input. Higher = more efficient.

## Combustion Air
Air supplied to a fuel-burning appliance for combustion. Required for safe operation.

## Compressor
Refrigeration cycle component that compresses refrigerant vapor, raising its temperature and pressure.

## Condenser
Refrigeration cycle component that releases heat from the refrigerant. Outdoor coil in cooling mode.

## Condensing Unit
Outdoor portion of split-system AC. Contains compressor + outdoor coil + outdoor fan.

## CSST
Corrugated Stainless Steel Tubing. Flexible gas piping. Requires special bonding.

## Defrost
Process where heat pump reverses temporarily to melt ice on the outdoor coil.

## Demand Defrost
Modern controls that initiate defrost based on actual ice accumulation, not just time.

## Direct Vent
Combustion appliance that draws combustion air from outside and exhausts directly outside.

## Duct
Channel that conveys air from blower to registers (and back).

## ECM
Electronically Commutated Motor. Variable-speed motor used in modern HVAC blowers.

## EER
Energy Efficiency Ratio at 95°F outdoor / 80°F indoor. Instantaneous efficiency value.

## Evaporator
Refrigeration cycle component that absorbs heat from the air. Indoor coil in cooling mode.

## Heat Pump
Refrigeration system that can run in cooling or heating mode by reversing the refrigeration cycle.

## HSPF
Heating Seasonal Performance Factor. Heat pump heating efficiency rating over a season.

## HVAC
Heating, Ventilation, Air Conditioning.

## IECC
International Energy Conservation Code. Sets building energy efficiency standards.

## IFGC
International Fuel Gas Code. Governs gas piping and appliances.

## IMC
International Mechanical Code. Governs HVAC installations.

## Manual J
ACCA procedure for residential load calculation.

## Manual D
ACCA procedure for residential duct design.

## Manual S
ACCA procedure for equipment selection matching load and ducts.

## Reversing Valve
Component in heat pumps that reverses refrigerant flow to switch between heating and cooling modes.

## SEER
Seasonal Energy Efficiency Ratio. Cooling efficiency over a typical season (pre-2023 standard).

## SEER2
Updated SEER standard (2023+) using more realistic test conditions.

## SHGC
Solar Heat Gain Coefficient. Fraction of solar energy passing through a window. Lower = less solar gain.

## Static Pressure
Resistance the blower must overcome to move air through the system. Measured in inches W.C.

## Subcooling
Temperature difference between condenser saturation temp and liquid line temp. Indicates proper refrigerant charge.

## Superheat
Temperature difference between evaporator outlet temp and saturation temp. Indicates proper refrigerant charge.

## Tonnage
HVAC capacity in tons. 1 ton = 12,000 BTU/hr (the heat required to melt 1 ton of ice in 24 hours).

## TXV
Thermostatic Expansion Valve. Modulates refrigerant flow into the evaporator based on superheat.

## U-Factor
Heat transmission through a building component. Lower = better insulation. (Inverse of R-value.)

## VFD
Variable Frequency Drive. Electronic motor controller used in commercial HVAC.

## Vent
Pipe that carries combustion exhaust outside the building.

## Variable-Speed
Equipment that operates at multiple speeds rather than just on/off. More efficient and quieter.
