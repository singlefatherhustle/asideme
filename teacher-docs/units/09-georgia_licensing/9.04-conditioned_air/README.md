# HVAC and Conditioned Air — Georgia Study Guide

**Unit:** Georgia Licensing
**Topic:** Conditioned Air (HVAC)

---

## Scope, Prerequisites, and What the Board Expects

### License classes

- **Conditioned Air Class I** — restricted to systems or equipment **not exceeding 175,000 BTU of heating and 60,000 BTU of cooling**.
- **Conditioned Air Class II** — **unrestricted**.

The rule also says that **for classification purposes, each complete system in a single installation is treated as an individual job**. That is a very examable detail because it affects whether a Class I contractor may legally handle a multi-system installation.

### To obtain the license

The applicant must:
- Submit a completed application
- Meet experience prerequisites
- Score at least **70** on the exam
- Pay the fees
- Provide **three references** from an architect, professional engineer, inspector, or licensed conditioned air contractor

The current SOS FAQ adds several documents candidates should expect in practice:
- A secure and verifiable document
- **EPA certification** (Type II or higher)
- A background check
- Proof of the **Manuals J & D** course
- For **nonrestricted/Class II applicants**, proof of **Manuals N & Q** or **Carrier Design 1, 2, & 3**

### Experience requirements

| Class | Years | Required experience mix |
|---|---|---|
| Class I | 4 years | Residential installation as lead mechanic, service-technician experience, evidence of EPA certification, residential supervisory experience, completion of board-approved heat-loss/gain and duct-design course |
| Class II | 5 years | More commercial emphasis and experience with systems above the Class I thresholds |

On the exam, this usually shows up as "Which applicant qualifies?" or "Which license is needed for this job?"

## Step-by-Step Load and Duct Workflow

A reliable exam and field workflow for a residential conditioned-air design problem:

1. **Gather building data** — orientation, insulation, window type, infiltration assumptions, occupancy, internal gains.
2. **Run Manual J** to separate sensible and latent loads room by room.
3. **Select equipment** that matches the calculated load (not the home's old nameplate size).
4. **Determine required airflow** by tonnage and room load allocation.
5. **Use Manual D** to calculate total effective length, friction rate, and branch sizes.
6. **Verify code items** — return-air strategy, condensate disposal, equipment disconnects, gas sizing/venting, electrical protection.
7. **Final-check the installation** against the applicable Georgia code set and local amendments.

The most common real-life failure is **not "bad math"** — it is installing equipment first and doing the design second.

## Sample Exam-Style Questions with Answers

**Q:** A contractor wants to replace a system rated at 200,000 BTU heat and 60,000 BTU cool under a Class I conditioned-air license. Is that allowed?
**A:** **No.** Class I is restricted to systems not exceeding **175,000 BTU of heating** and **60,000 BTU of cooling**. The heating side exceeds the Class I ceiling.

**Q:** Which extra documentation is specifically called out for Georgia nonrestricted/Class II conditioned-air applicants in the current FAQ?
**A:** Proof of completion for **Manuals N & Q** or **Carrier Design 1, 2, & 3**, in addition to the other required materials.

**Q:** What happens after a Georgia conditioned-air candidate fails the exam twice?
**A:** The candidate must complete a **board-approved review course** before sitting again.

## Real-Life Scenario Questions with Answers

**Scenario:** A Class I contractor is asked to install three separate split systems in one large custom home. Each complete system is below 175,000 BTU heat and 60,000 BTU cool.
**Answer:** The rule says that for classifying systems, **each complete system in a single installation is considered an individual job**. That makes the classification analysis **system-by-system** rather than by adding all equipment tonnage together.

**Scenario:** A candidate has strong install experience but never completed the required heat-loss/gain and duct-design coursework.
**Answer:** That candidate is **still incomplete**. Georgia's conditioned-air rule and FAQ both make the board-approved design-course completion part of the qualification path.
