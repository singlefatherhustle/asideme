# Agency Disclosure Requirements

## When disclosure is required

Georgia license law requires written agency disclosure at the **first substantive contact** between an agent and a customer or client.

```
First substantive contact = beyond pleasantries; entering into 
discussion of specific property, terms, or representation
```

Examples that trigger disclosure:

- Buyer asks the agent to show properties
- Seller calls to discuss listing
- Buyer asks about financing through the broker
- Discussion of price ranges or specific properties

NOT triggering disclosure:

- Saying hello at an open house
- General market chitchat at a community event
- Brief casual questions ("nice neighborhood?")

## The Brokerage Engagement Agreement

Georgia uses a Brokerage Engagement Agreement (BEA) as the formal contract between client and broker:

- **Listing Brokerage Engagement** (LBE): seller engages broker to list/sell their property
- **Buyer Brokerage Engagement** (BBE): buyer engages broker to find/buy property

The BEA is the formal client contract. It specifies:

- Duration of the engagement
- Compensation terms
- Termination conditions
- Agent's obligations
- Client's obligations

## Customer disclosure

When dealing with someone who is NOT your client (a customer), disclose:

- That you represent the other party (or no one)
- The nature of the relationship
- What duties the customer can expect from you (some duties to all, like honesty)
- What duties the customer CANNOT expect (advocacy, confidentiality)

This is often done verbally at first contact, then confirmed in writing through a customer disclosure form.

## Sample disclosure language

```
"I represent [the seller / no one]. The brokerage I work with is 
Smith Realty. I cannot advocate against the seller for you, 
but I can provide you with property information and 
facilitate showings. If you'd like representation, you should 
consider engaging a buyer's agent."
```

## Dual agency disclosure

For dual agency to be legal:

- Written disclosure BEFORE acting as dual agent
- Both parties consent in writing
- Specific dual agency disclosure form used (Georgia REALTOR form GA-001A)
- Limits of dual agency clearly stated:
  - No price negotiation between parties
  - No disclosure of confidential information
  - No advocacy for either side

## Designated agency disclosure

Designated agency:

- Each party signs a disclosure recognizing the firm represents both sides
- Specific agent designated for each party
- Broker oversees but doesn't represent either personally
- Both parties consent in writing

## Timing of disclosures

| Event | Disclosure |
|---|---|
| First contact (broad) | Agency type (verbal + written) |
| Engaging as client | BEA signed |
| Becoming dual agent | Dual agency disclosure signed |
| Material conflict develops | Update disclosure if needed |
| Termination | Written confirmation if applicable |

## Failure to disclose

| Violation | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Failure to disclose to client (you don't have a contract) | Warning + CE |
| Failure to disclose to other party (acted as if for them) | Probation |
| Acting as dual agent without consent | License suspension |
| Misrepresenting agency relationship | Revocation in severe cases |

## Common disclosure errors

- Verbal disclosure only (not documented in writing)
- Forgetting to update when relationships change
- Acting as dual agent without dual agency disclosure
- Letting a customer "feel" like a client without correction
- Disclosing on the day of closing (too late)
