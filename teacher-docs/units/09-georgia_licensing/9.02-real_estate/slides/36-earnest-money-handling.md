# Earnest Money Handling

## What earnest money is

A buyer's deposit indicating good-faith intent to complete a purchase. The amount is negotiated in the offer (typically 1-2% of purchase price for residential).

## Timing requirements — Georgia

The broker must deposit earnest money into the trust account:

- **Within 3 banking days** of receipt (Georgia rule)
- Saturdays, Sundays, holidays don't count
- Receipt date is when the check arrives or wire is received

```
Day 1: Buyer signs offer, gives check
Day 2: Listing agent has check (forwarded by buyer's agent)
Day 3: Broker has check
Day 4: Banking day 1
Day 5: Banking day 2
Day 6: Banking day 3 — MUST be deposited by end of day
```

## How to handle the check

1. Hold-out / "good faith deposit": placed in trust pending acceptance
2. Negotiable instrument (check): deposit to trust within 3 banking days
3. Wire transfer: confirmed receipt = deposit clock starts

## Refundable vs non-refundable

The contract specifies:

```
- Refundable: returnable if certain contingencies aren't met
- Non-refundable: forfeited if buyer backs out for any reason

OR a hybrid:
- Refundable during inspection contingency (typically 10-14 days)
- Non-refundable after that point
```

## Refund disbursement

When earnest money is refundable and the buyer is owed it back:

- **Both parties must agree** to the disbursement in writing
- Cannot disburse on one party's say-so alone
- If parties disagree, the broker holds the funds until resolved
- May escrow with a third party if dispute persists

## Forfeiture disbursement

When earnest money is forfeited:

- Both parties must agree (typically the seller gets it)
- Contract specifies how it's split between seller and broker
- The broker may NOT keep more than allowed by contract

```
Typical forfeiture split (Georgia residential):
   60% to seller
   40% to listing broker (or as negotiated)
```

## Disputed earnest money

If buyer and seller dispute who is owed the deposit:

- Broker is **not** the arbitrator
- Broker holds the funds in trust pending resolution
- Resolution options: mutual release, mediation, arbitration, court order

**A broker who disburses disputed funds before resolution is personally liable** for the wrong disbursement.

## Documentation

Every trust account transaction must be documented:

- Deposit slip / wire confirmation
- Detailed ledger entry: who, what, when, why
- Buyer's name, seller's name, property address, contract date
- Account statement reconciled monthly

## Common earnest money errors

- Holding the deposit personally (commingling)
- Disbursing without both parties' consent
- Not depositing within 3 banking days
- Failing to document who paid the deposit
- Forgetting to refund a "refundable" deposit when the buyer is entitled
- Disbursing to the broker's operating account instead of trust until cleared
