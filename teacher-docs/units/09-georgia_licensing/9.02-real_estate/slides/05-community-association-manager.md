# Community Association Manager (CAM)

## What CAM is

A Community Association Manager license authorizes professional management of:

- Condominium associations
- Homeowner associations (HOAs)
- Planned communities
- Resort properties
- Townhome associations
- Some commercial property associations

This is a **separate license track** from sales licenses. A CAM cannot list or sell property unless they also hold a sales license.

## Why CAM exists separately

Real estate sales and association management are different services:

- **Sales** = brokering transactions between owners and buyers
- **CAM** = managing day-to-day operations of an existing community

Florida and Nevada pioneered CAM licensing; Georgia follows similar patterns.

## Application requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|---|---|
| Age | At least 18 |
| Pre-license education | Approved CAM course (specific hours) |
| Application fee | Set by GREC |
| Background check | Yes |
| Sponsor | Working under a CAM-licensed firm or self-employed (depending on regulation) |

The CAM-specific course covers:

- Association law (condo, HOA acts)
- Financial management of association funds
- Reserve studies
- Meeting management
- Board interaction
- Bylaws and CC&Rs interpretation
- Maintenance contract management

## CAM activities

Day-to-day work includes:

- Collecting dues and assessments
- Managing the association's bank accounts
- Coordinating maintenance vendors
- Conducting board meetings
- Preparing budgets and reserve fund analyses
- Handling owner complaints and architectural review
- Issuing violation notices per CC&Rs
- Coordinating elections

## CAM trust account requirements

Like brokers, CAMs hold association funds in trust:

- Separate trust account per association (or pooled with specific accounting)
- Quarterly reconciliation
- Audit annually (varies by association size)
- Strict commingling prohibition

## CAM vs Property Manager

In Georgia:

| Role | Requires license |
|---|---|
| Manage a single rental property | No (owner can hire anyone) |
| Manage rental properties for multiple owners | Real estate license + property management agreement |
| Manage condo/HOA association | **CAM license** |

The distinction is whether you're managing a single property or an entire community of multiple separately-owned units.

## License renewal

CAM licenses renew every 2 years with continuing education requirements specific to community association management.

## Career pattern

CAMs typically have:

- Background in finance, accounting, or property management
- Strong soft skills (board diplomacy is constant)
- Vendor management experience
- Some real estate sales practitioners cross-train into CAM as a niche

## Common CAM violations

- Operating without active license
- Commingling association funds with personal accounts
- Skipping reserve fund analysis
- Failing to provide required disclosures to unit buyers
- Inadequate board meeting documentation
