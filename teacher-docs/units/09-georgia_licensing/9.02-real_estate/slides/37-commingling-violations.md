# Commingling Violations

## What commingling is

Mixing the broker's personal or business funds with funds held in trust for others. The line is absolute — even temporary commingling is a violation.

## Common forms

### 1. Personal funds in trust account
```
Broker writes a business check from the trust account to pay for office supplies.
→ Commingling
```

### 2. Trust funds in operating account
```
Earnest money deposit accidentally goes to the operating account.
→ Commingling
```

### 3. Commission earned but not drawn
```
Closing occurs; trust holds purchase funds.
Broker takes commission directly without first transferring to operating account.
→ Risk of commingling if procedure isn't documented
```

### 4. Brokerage funds in same account as trust
```
Single bank account holds both trust funds and operating funds.
→ Commingling — even with internal accounting, the funds are mixed in the bank's record.
```

## The Georgia rule

O.C.G.A. § 43-40-25(b)(8) requires:

```
"A broker shall maintain trust funds in a separate trust account
 in a bank or financial institution... and shall not commingle
 such funds with personal funds or any business funds of the broker."
```

The statute is unambiguous. There is no "minor commingling" exception in Georgia law.

## How GREC detects commingling

- Trust account audit (random or triggered by complaint)
- Reconciliation showing balance off by even a small amount
- Bank account statements reviewed
- Records cross-referenced with transaction logs

## Penalties

| Severity | Penalty |
|---|---|
| Minor commingling, quickly corrected, no harm | Warning + CE requirement |
| Moderate commingling | Probation + restricted license |
| Repeated commingling | License suspension |
| Commingling combined with conversion | License revocation + civil/criminal liability |

## Real-world scenarios

**Scenario 1**: Earnest money deposit is wired to the wrong account.

```
Broker discovers within 24 hours and transfers funds back.
GREC review: minor administrative error, no harm.
Outcome: warning, possibly required CE on trust accounting.
```

**Scenario 2**: Broker pays office rent from trust account.

```
GREC review: deliberate commingling, even if temporary.
Outcome: probation, license restriction, fine.
```

**Scenario 3**: Broker pays personal cell phone bill from trust account.

```
GREC review: personal use of trust funds = both commingling AND conversion.
Outcome: license suspension, possible revocation.
```

## Best practices to avoid commingling

1. **Separate accounts**: trust account at one bank, operating at another (or at least clearly different accounts at the same bank)
2. **Different signatories** if possible, or strict policies on transfers
3. **Monthly reconciliation** with documented sign-off
4. **No commingling even temporarily** — never write a single check that crosses the line
5. **Annual or quarterly audits** by an outside CPA for larger firms
6. **Written procedures** in firm operations manual
7. **Train all staff** on trust account rules

## Common mistakes

- Treating trust funds as "the broker's money to manage" rather than fiduciary
- Allowing salespersons to make trust account deposits without broker review
- Mixing earnest money and commission funds before transaction closes
- Late reconciliation missing a commingling event
- Failing to document transfers between accounts
