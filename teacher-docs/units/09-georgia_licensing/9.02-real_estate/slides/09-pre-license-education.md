# Pre-License Education Requirements

## Salesperson — 75 hours

Georgia requires 75 hours of approved pre-license education for the salesperson license. Topics covered:

| Hours | Topic |
|---|---|
| 25 | Real estate principles + practice |
| 15 | Real estate law (Georgia-specific) |
| 12 | Finance and economics |
| 8 | Agency relationships and fiduciary duties |
| 8 | Contracts and closing process |
| 5 | Fair housing and ethics |
| 2 | Math fundamentals for real estate |

## Broker — additional 60 hours

To upgrade to broker, an additional 60 hours of advanced education:

| Hours | Topic |
|---|---|
| 15 | Broker / firm operation |
| 12 | Advanced real estate law |
| 10 | Trust accounts and financial management |
| 8 | Investment property and commercial |
| 8 | Property management |
| 5 | Risk management and ethics |
| 2 | Math advanced calculations |

## Approved providers

GREC maintains a list of approved education providers:

- **Career WebSchool** (online)
- **Real Estate Institute** (in-person + online)
- **The CE Shop** (online)
- **Kaplan Real Estate Education** (online + in-person)
- **Bert Rodgers Schools** (online + in-person)
- **Local community colleges** with approved programs

Verify current approval before enrolling. Unapproved hours don't count.

## Course format options

- **Online self-paced**: most popular, $200-500 typical cost, you progress at your own rate
- **Online instructor-led**: scheduled video classes, more interaction, ~$500-1000
- **In-person**: traditional classroom, ~$800-1500
- **Hybrid**: mix of online + in-person sessions

## Course completion requirement

After completing the hours:

- Pass the course exam (provided by the school)
- Receive a course completion certificate
- Certificate is required when applying for the GREC exam
- Certificate is valid for **1 year** after issuance

## Common pre-license errors

- Enrolling in a non-approved course (hours don't count)
- Forgetting to apply for exam before certificate expires
- Skipping the math review (math is on the exam)
- Choosing online when learning style requires structured class
- Underestimating time: 75 hours is real study time, not just "watching videos"

## Pre-license vs CE

These are different:

- **Pre-license**: required BEFORE you can sit the licensing exam
- **CE (continuing education)**: required AFTER licensing, every 2 years for renewal

You don't double-count hours between them.

## Cost comparison

```
Cheapest path (DIY online): $200-400
Mid-range (online + textbook): $400-600
Premium (in-person + tutor): $1000-2000
```

Average pre-license cost: $300-500. Add $98 exam fee + $125 initial license = $500-700 total upfront before earning first commission.
