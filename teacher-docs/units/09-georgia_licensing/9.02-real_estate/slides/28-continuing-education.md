# Continuing Education

## The renewal cycle

After your initial 4-year term, Georgia real estate licenses renew **every 2 years**. Each 2-year cycle requires:

- **9 hours** of approved continuing education
- **At least 3 hours** must be on a specific GREC-mandated topic (varies by cycle)
- Completion certificates uploaded or available for audit
- Renewal fee paid

## Mandatory topics

GREC designates specific topics every 2-year cycle that all licensees must cover. Past examples:

- License law updates (3 hours)
- Trust account compliance (3 hours)
- Agency relationships (3 hours)
- Fair housing (3 hours)

The remaining 6 hours can be elective from the approved CE catalog.

## What counts as CE

| Activity | Counts? |
|---|---|
| Approved CE courses (online or in-person) | Yes |
| GREC-approved seminars | Yes |
| Online webinars from approved providers | Yes |
| Conference attendance (with approved CE credit) | Yes |
| Bar association CLE on real estate topics | Sometimes (check approval) |
| Self-study books or videos | **No** (unless part of approved course) |
| On-the-job training | No |
| Time spent reading the Georgia license law | No |

## How CE differs from pre-license

Pre-license = 75 hours, BEFORE getting licensed
Post-license = 25 hours, FIRST year of licensure
CE = 9 hours every 2 years AFTER the initial term

These are separate requirements. Hours from one don't count toward another.

## Approved CE providers

Same general list as pre-license:

- Career WebSchool
- The CE Shop
- Kaplan
- Bert Rodgers
- Real Estate Institute
- Various individual instructors and brokerages

Many courses are now offered online with self-paced format. Some sessions have specific completion deadlines.

## CE for broker

Brokers have:

- Same 9-hour requirement
- Slightly different mandatory topics (broker-focused)
- Some broker-only courses available

## Tracking your hours

GREC requires you to maintain records of your CE hours. Best practices:

- Print or save completion certificates from each course
- Keep them in a file (paper or digital) for at least 5 years
- Note which mandatory topic was covered
- Track total hours per cycle

GREC random audits these records. If audited and records are incomplete, license may be suspended pending completion verification.

## Online vs in-person CE

| Format | Pros | Cons |
|---|---|---|
| Online self-paced | Flexible, often cheaper | Easy to procrastinate |
| Online instructor-led | Interactive, scheduled | Less flexibility |
| In-person | Networking, hands-on practice | Most expensive, time-consuming |

Most working agents use online for 6 of the 9 hours and in-person for the most complex topic.

## Common CE errors

- Cramming all 9 hours at the end of the cycle
- Missing the mandatory topic for the cycle
- Using non-approved provider
- Letting renewal lapse and assuming you can backfill CE later (you can, but with fees)
- Not retaining certificates (auditable)
