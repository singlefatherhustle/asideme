# Georgia Real Estate License Classes

## Three classifications

The Georgia Real Estate Commission (GREC) administers four distinct license types:

| License | Scope |
|---|---|
| **Salesperson** | Must work under a broker; entry-level license |
| **Associate Broker** | Holds a broker qualification but practices under a brokerage firm |
| **Broker** | May practice independently, supervise others, operate a brokerage |
| **Community Association Manager (CAM)** | Manages condominiums, HOAs, planned communities |

## The career path

```
Salesperson → (3 years + courses) → Associate Broker
                                         ↓
                                       Broker (own firm)
```

Most practitioners enter as **Salesperson** and decide whether to advance based on their goals (working under a brokerage vs operating independently).

## Salesperson scope

What a Georgia real estate salesperson can do:

- Represent buyers and sellers in transactions
- Show properties
- Negotiate offers
- Handle earnest money (deposited in broker's trust account, not personal)
- Receive a commission only through their sponsoring broker

What they cannot do:

- Operate independently
- Hold their own trust account
- Sign listing agreements in their own name (must be in the brokerage's name)
- Hire other agents

## Broker scope

A Georgia broker may:

- Operate a brokerage independently
- Hire and supervise salespersons and associate brokers
- Maintain trust accounts
- Sign contracts on behalf of the brokerage
- Advertise services
- Receive commissions directly

## Associate Broker

A licensee who has earned the broker qualification (3+ years experience, broker course, broker exam) but **chooses to remain affiliated with a brokerage**. Common for:

- High-producing agents who don't want to manage a firm
- Agents waiting to start their own brokerage
- Family-business arrangements

## Community Association Manager

Specialized license for managing:

- Condominium associations
- Homeowner associations (HOAs)
- Planned communities
- Resort properties

Does not require a real estate sales license unless also brokering property sales.

## Common path errors

- Practicing without active affiliation to a broker (illegal for salesperson)
- Salesperson holding earnest money in personal account (commingling — license suspension)
- Broker letting their license lapse while running a firm (firm operates unlicensed)
- Advertising services without proper license disclosure
