# Sample Question — Agency Relationships

## The question

> An agent at ABC Realty was hired by John to represent him in buying a house. While viewing properties, the agent learns that John's wife is being unfaithful and might leave him before closing. The agent should:

```
A) Tell the seller, who has the right to know about potential 
   buyer issues
B) Tell their broker so the broker can decide what to disclose
C) Maintain confidentiality, even if it means John doesn't 
   purchase the house
D) Mention it offhandedly to a colleague but not to anyone official
```

## Step-by-step analysis

### Apply the OLD CAR mnemonic

- **O — Obedience**: John wants to find and buy a house. Agent is not instructed to disclose marital issues.
- **L — Loyalty**: Agent's duty is to John's interests, not the seller's.
- **D — Disclosure**: Material facts about the PROPERTY must be disclosed to John, not to the seller about John.
- **C — Confidentiality**: This is the key duty here. Personal information about the client must remain confidential.
- **A — Accounting**: Not relevant to this question.
- **R — Reasonable Care**: Disclosing personal info would harm the client.

### The correct answer

**C) Maintain confidentiality, even if it means John doesn't purchase the house.**

The agent owes John the duty of confidentiality. Information about John's marriage is:

- Not material to the property
- Personal and private
- Not disclosable to the other side

If John's wife actually leaves and the deal falls through, the agent's loyalty is to John, not to making the deal happen.

## Why each wrong answer is wrong

**A) Tell the seller**: Violates loyalty AND confidentiality. The seller is the customer, not the client. Telling the seller about John's marital problems serves the seller's interest at John's expense.

**B) Tell their broker**: Even within the brokerage, this information is not relevant to the broker's supervisory role. The agent should not share confidential client information broadly.

**D) Mention to a colleague**: Casual sharing of confidential information is a breach. Even if no one else is told, the agent has violated trust by chatting about it.

## Related concept

A common variant of this question:

> The agent learns that the buyer plans to use the property as a marijuana grow operation (illegal in Georgia). The agent should:

In this case, the agent has a DIFFERENT obligation:

- Cannot facilitate illegal activity
- Must address with the client
- May need to withdraw from representation if client insists on illegal use
- Should consult their broker
- The fiduciary duty of loyalty does NOT extend to assisting illegal acts

The distinction: confidentiality protects legal personal information. It does not protect plans for illegal activity.

## The general rule

```
Personal info about client → confidential (protect)
Material property facts → disclose
Plans for illegal activity → cannot facilitate
Client's specific preferences → confidential to client, can use to find matches
```
