# Pre-License Course Content Overview

## The 75-hour breakdown

Georgia pre-license courses cover seven core areas. The actual content depth varies by provider, but all must hit these topics:

### 1. Real Estate Principles (~15 hours)

- Property ownership and rights
- Real estate market dynamics
- Types of property (residential, commercial, agricultural)
- Land descriptions and surveying basics
- Property characteristics

### 2. Real Estate Law (Georgia-specific) (~15 hours)

- Georgia Real Estate License Law (Title 43, Chapter 40)
- GREC rules and regulations
- Property rights under Georgia law
- Eminent domain
- Adverse possession
- Property records and recording

### 3. Real Estate Finance (~12 hours)

- Mortgage types and lending process
- Loan-to-value ratios
- Debt-to-income calculations
- FHA, VA, conventional, USDA loans
- Interest rates and amortization
- Property taxes
- Insurance (title, homeowner's, mortgage)

### 4. Agency Relationships (~8 hours)

- Types of agency
- Fiduciary duties (OLD CAR)
- Agency disclosure requirements
- Customer vs client
- Termination of agency
- Agency forms

### 5. Contracts and Closing (~8 hours)

- Statute of frauds
- Real estate sales contract elements
- Contingencies (financing, inspection, appraisal)
- Closing process
- HUD-1 and Closing Disclosure
- Title transfer
- Recording

### 6. Fair Housing and Ethics (~5 hours)

- Federal Fair Housing Act
- Georgia Fair Housing Law
- Protected classes
- Disparate impact
- Code of Ethics (REALTOR if applicable)
- Anti-discrimination practices

### 7. Math Fundamentals (~2 hours)

- Commission calculations
- Property tax math
- Mortgage payment math
- Square footage calculations
- Investment property analysis basics

## What's heavily tested

The exam emphasizes:

```
1. Agency relationships (8 hrs of content, ~25% of exam questions)
2. Contracts and closing (8 hrs, ~20% of questions)
3. Trust accounts and finance (12 hrs, ~15% of questions)
4. Georgia license law (15 hrs, ~15% of questions)
5. Fair housing (5 hrs, ~10% of questions)
6. Property rights/law (15 hrs, ~10% of questions)
7. Math (2 hrs, ~5% of questions)
```

## Study time allocation

```
Total course hours: 75
Recommended study time on top: 30-50 hours
   - Re-read complex topics
   - Practice questions
   - Memorize key vocabulary
   - Math problems repetition
```

Total time invested before sitting exam: 100-125 hours typical.

## Why the math section is small but critical

Only 2 hours of class time but commonly 4-6 exam questions. Each math question is worth points and is almost always answerable IF you know the formulas:

```
Commission = Sale price × Commission rate × Split %
Property tax = Assessed value × Mill rate / 1000
Mortgage payment = Use amortization table or calculator
```

Many candidates skip the math because the section is short. Don't.

## What's NOT covered in pre-license

Pre-license doesn't deeply cover:

- Property management operations
- Commercial real estate analysis
- Investment property syndication
- Real estate development
- Foreclosure law (some coverage)
- Commercial leasing

These require post-license courses or specialty certifications.
