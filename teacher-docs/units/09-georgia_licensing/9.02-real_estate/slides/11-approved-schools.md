# Approved Schools and Providers

## What "approved" means

GREC maintains an official list of approved pre-license and continuing education providers. Only hours from approved providers count toward licensing requirements.

## How to verify approval

```
1. Visit GREC website
2. Check "Approved Schools" or "Pre-License Provider List"
3. Confirm provider has current approval status
4. Note any specific course offerings approved (some providers only approved for certain hours)
```

## What to look for in a provider

- **Pass rate** for their students on the licensing exam
- **Refund policy** if you don't complete or fail
- **Practice exams** included or extra
- **Live support** vs purely automated
- **Mobile access** for studying on the go
- **Updates** to material when GREC rules change

## Common pre-license course red flags

- Provider not on current GREC approved list
- Pricing too cheap to be substantive ($50 for 75 hours of content is suspicious)
- No practice exams or quizzes
- "Guaranteed to pass" claims (no school can guarantee this)
- Outdated material (older than 2 years)

## Online vs in-person

Pros of online:
- Self-paced, fit your schedule
- Often cheaper
- Replay sections you struggle with
- Available 24/7

Pros of in-person:
- Direct instructor interaction
- Networking with classmates
- Forced commitment / pacing
- Hands-on practice with contracts

Hybrid (online + occasional in-person) often gets the best results.

## After completing the course

The provider issues a **course completion certificate**:

- Valid for 1 year from issue
- Required when applying to take the exam
- Provider keeps records (in case you lose your copy)
- GREC may verify directly with the school

Lose the certificate? Contact the school — they can reissue, sometimes for a small fee.

## Specific accommodations

If you have disabilities or learning differences:

- Most approved online providers comply with ADA
- Request accommodations before purchasing
- Some courses offer audio versions for visually impaired
- Reasonable accommodations available for those with learning disabilities

## Common errors

- Enrolling in non-approved school (hours don't count)
- Using expired (1+ year old) certificate to apply
- Mixing pre-license with CE hours (different requirements)
- Not requesting accommodations until after enrollment
