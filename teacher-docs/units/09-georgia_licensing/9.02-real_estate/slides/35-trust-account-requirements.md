# Trust Account Requirements

## What a trust account is

A trust account holds **other people's money** — earnest money, rental security deposits, escrow funds — separate from the brokerage's operating funds. The broker is the fiduciary; the funds belong to the buyers, sellers, tenants, or third parties.

## Georgia rule (O.C.G.A. § 43-40-20)

Every real estate broker who handles trust funds must:

- Maintain a separate trust account at a Georgia-chartered or federally-insured bank
- Account name must include "Trust Account" or "Escrow Account"
- Funds must be held until the transaction closes or the deposit is properly disbursed
- Personal or business funds must NEVER be mixed with trust funds (commingling)

## Account naming

The trust account must be styled:

```
ABC Realty Trust Account
Account ending: XXXX
Bank: XYZ Bank N.A.
Account holder: [Brokerage Name] [Trust Account / Escrow Account]
```

The account name must clearly indicate it is held in trust.

## Who can authorize disbursements

For each trust account:

- The qualifying broker has primary authority
- Up to one additional licensee may be authorized in writing
- The broker is responsible for ALL disbursements, even if delegated

## What can be in trust

Real estate-related funds only:

```
Allowed:
  - Earnest money deposits
  - Rental security deposits
  - First-month rent / last-month rent
  - Pet deposits
  - Cleaning deposits
  - Construction deposits in escrow
  - Trust funds from estate sales held by the brokerage

Not allowed:
  - Personal funds of any kind
  - Operating capital of the brokerage
  - Commissions earned but not yet drawn
  - Loans to the brokerage
  - Investment funds unrelated to a transaction
```

## Reconciliation

Brokers must:

- Reconcile the trust account **monthly** at minimum
- Document the reconciliation in writing
- Match the bank balance + outstanding deposits/withdrawals = the trust ledger
- Retain reconciliation records for 5 years

Discrepancies (even $1) must be investigated and resolved immediately.

## Audit triggers

GREC may audit a trust account if:

- A complaint is filed about a transaction
- A licensee surrenders or transfers the firm
- The broker is under disciplinary review
- A random audit (rare but possible)

Inadequate records lead to license suspension or revocation.

## Common trust account violations

- **Commingling**: putting brokerage funds in trust account, or vice versa
- **Conversion**: using trust funds for personal expenses
- **Inadequate reconciliation**: missing monthly reconciliation
- **Improper account naming**: not labeled as trust/escrow
- **Disbursement without authority**: paying out before the transaction closes (without all parties' consent)

## Disciplinary consequences

| Violation | Typical penalty |
|---|---|
| Minor commingling (small amount, quickly corrected) | Warning + CE requirement |
| Significant commingling | Probation + license restriction |
| Conversion (using funds for self) | License revocation + criminal referral |
| Repeated trust account violations | Permanent ineligibility |

Real estate trust account violations are taken very seriously — they undermine the entire industry's credibility with the public.
