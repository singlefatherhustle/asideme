# Glossary — Key Real Estate Terms

## Agency
The legal relationship between a broker and a client. The broker acts on the client's behalf. See: Single Agency, Dual Agency, Designated Agency.

## Agency Disclosure
Written notification to all parties in a transaction about whom the broker represents. Required at first substantive contact.

## Appraisal
Professional opinion of property's market value, usually performed by a licensed appraiser. Required by most lenders.

## Brokerage Engagement Agreement (BEA)
Written contract between client and brokerage establishing the agency relationship. Includes Listing BEA (seller) and Buyer BEA.

## Closing
The final transfer of ownership from seller to buyer. Funds are disbursed, title is recorded.

## Closing Disclosure (CD)
Federal form required by RESPA showing all costs and fees of a residential closing. Delivered to buyer 3 business days before closing.

## Commingling
Mixing client trust funds with personal or business funds. Major violation in real estate.

## Contingency
A condition that must be met for a contract to remain enforceable. Common types: financing, inspection, appraisal, title.

## Conversion
Using trust funds for purposes other than the client's purpose. Theft, essentially. License revocation.

## Customer
A party in a transaction with whom the broker has no agency relationship. Common in buyer/seller transactions where each has separate representation.

## Designated Agency
Different agents within the same firm representing buyer and seller. The qualifying broker oversees both.

## Disclosure
Communicating material facts to the relevant parties. Property defects, agency relationships, conflicts of interest.

## Dual Agency
The same broker representing both buyer and seller in a single transaction. Requires written informed consent from both parties.

## Earnest Money
Buyer's good-faith deposit indicating intent to complete the purchase. Held in broker's trust account until closing or other disposition.

## Equity
Property value minus outstanding mortgage debt. The owner's actual financial interest.

## Escrow
Funds held by a third party (usually the broker or title company) until conditions are met. Earnest money is held in escrow.

## Fair Housing Act
Federal law (Title VIII) prohibiting discrimination in housing based on protected classes (race, color, national origin, religion, sex, familial status, disability).

## Fiduciary Duty
Legal obligation to act in the client's best interest. OLD CAR mnemonic: Obedience, Loyalty, Disclosure, Confidentiality, Accounting, Reasonable Care.

## Foreclosure
Legal process where lender takes possession of mortgaged property when borrower defaults. Georgia uses non-judicial foreclosure (faster than judicial).

## GREC
Georgia Real Estate Commission. State agency administering real estate licensing.

## Hard Money
Loans from private lenders, often at higher interest rates, used for short-term real estate investment. Different from conventional mortgages.

## HUD-1 / Closing Disclosure
Historic form (HUD-1) replaced by Closing Disclosure for most residential transactions under TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure (TRID) rule.

## Listing Broker
Broker who represents the seller. Has the listing agreement.

## Listing Brokerage Engagement
Written agreement between seller and brokerage to list and market the property.

## Material Fact
Any fact that would reasonably affect a party's decision in the transaction. Defects, contingencies, financial issues, etc.

## Mortgage
Loan secured by real estate. Provides funds for purchase or refinance.

## Multiple Listing Service (MLS)
Database of properties listed by participating brokers. Allows wide exposure to all member brokers and their clients.

## PMI / MIP
Private Mortgage Insurance / Mortgage Insurance Premium. Required when borrower puts less than 20% down on conventional / FHA loans.

## Pre-license Education
Required hours of approved education (75 for salesperson, additional 60 for broker) before sitting the licensing exam.

## Property Tax
Annual tax based on assessed property value. Set by local taxing authorities. Paid through escrow or directly by owner.

## Realtor
A licensed real estate professional who is a member of the National Association of REALTORS. NOT a generic term for all licensees.

## Reconciliation
Process of matching trust account balance with detailed transaction records. Required monthly for brokers.

## Recording
Filing a deed or other document with the county clerk's office to establish public record of ownership.

## RESPA
Real Estate Settlement Procedures Act. Federal law governing closing process, prohibits kickbacks, requires Good Faith Estimate (now Loan Estimate).

## Salesperson
Real estate licensee who works under a broker. Cannot operate independently.

## Statute of Frauds
Legal doctrine requiring certain contracts (including real estate) to be in writing to be enforceable.

## Subagent
A licensee or firm working with a buyer but representing the seller. Largely deprecated in favor of buyer agency or designated agency.

## Title
Legal ownership of property. Confirmed through title search and protected by title insurance.

## Trust Account
Separate bank account held by a broker for client funds (earnest money, deposits, etc.). Must be labeled as trust/escrow.

## Trust Fund
Money held in trust by a broker on behalf of clients. Must not be commingled with personal or business funds.

## TRID
TILA-RESPA Integrated Disclosure rule. Combined disclosure rules for residential mortgages. Requires Loan Estimate (LE) and Closing Disclosure (CD).

## VA Loan
Mortgage guaranteed by the Department of Veterans Affairs. Available to qualifying veterans, often with favorable terms.

## Yield Spread Premium
Compensation paid by a lender to a mortgage broker when the broker delivers a loan at a higher interest rate than the borrower could have qualified for.
