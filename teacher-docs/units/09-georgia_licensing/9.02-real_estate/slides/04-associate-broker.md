# Associate Broker

## What it is

An associate broker has earned the **broker qualification** (3 years experience as a licensee + broker course + passing broker exam) but **chooses to remain affiliated with a brokerage** rather than operating independently.

## Why someone becomes an associate broker

- **Higher commission split**: brokers can negotiate better terms with their firm
- **Easier path to opening their own firm later**: keeps broker qualification active
- **Higher reputation in transactions**: "broker" carries more weight than "salesperson"
- **Tax planning**: may be advantageous to operate as associate broker for self-employment
- **No firm responsibilities**: doesn't have to manage trust account, agents, etc.
- **Specialty**: focuses on high-end transactions where broker designation impresses

## Distinctions from a salesperson

| Aspect | Salesperson | Associate Broker |
|---|---|---|
| Education required | 75 hours pre-license | + 60 hours broker course |
| Experience needed | None to start | 3 years as licensee |
| Exam | Salesperson exam | Broker exam |
| Trust account | Cannot maintain | Cannot maintain (firm's account only) |
| Supervise other agents | No | Limited (firm-dependent) |
| Commission negotiation | Firm-set | More flexibility within firm |
| Sign listings in own name | No (firm's name) | Generally no (firm's name) |
| Independence | None | None — affiliated with firm |

## Distinction from independent broker

An associate broker:

- **Cannot** open their own firm without converting to independent broker status
- **Cannot** sign on behalf of their own firm (they don't have one)
- **Cannot** advertise as the "broker of record" of a firm they don't own

To upgrade to independent broker: notify GREC, possibly take additional course depending on time elapsed.

## How it looks in practice

```
ABC REALTY (firm)
   Qualifying Broker: Jane Smith
   
   Agents:
     Bob Jones (Salesperson)
     Mary Lee (Associate Broker)  ← higher tier than Bob
     Tom Ng (Associate Broker)    ← higher tier than Bob
```

Mary and Tom have the broker exam under their belt and a 3-year history, but they're not running the firm. Bob is supervised by Jane (the qualifying broker). Mary and Tom may also receive some supervisory duties from Jane.

## Compensation patterns

Associate brokers typically:

- Have a higher commission split (say 70/30 vs 60/40 for salespersons)
- May have a graduated commission structure (more deals = better split)
- Often own equity in the firm (in some partnerships)

## License renewal

Associate brokers renew on the same 2-year cycle as salespersons. CE requirements include the broker-track topics, slightly different from salesperson CE.

## Common associate broker errors

- Holding out as a broker independently (illegal — they're still affiliated)
- Operating a separate "team" or "DBA" that suggests an independent firm (regulated)
- Maintaining personal social media that doesn't disclose firm affiliation
- Signing offers in personal name vs firm name
