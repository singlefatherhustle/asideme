# Fiduciary Duties — OLD CAR

## The OLD CAR mnemonic

The six fiduciary duties a real estate agent owes to a client:

```
O — Obedience       (follow lawful client instructions)
L — Loyalty         (put client interests first)
D — Disclosure      (full disclosure of material facts)
C — Confidentiality (protect client confidences)
A — Accounting      (proper handling of client funds)
R — Reasonable Care (use professional skill and diligence)
```

## Obedience

The agent must:

- Follow the client's lawful instructions
- Refuse to follow unlawful instructions (refuse and document)
- Not impose the agent's preferences on the client

Example: client asks the agent to list at $400k. Agent thinks $375k would sell faster. Agent recommends but ultimately must list at $400k if that's what the client decides.

## Loyalty

The agent's primary obligation is the **client's interests**, above all others:

- Above the agent's own financial interest
- Above the firm's interest
- Above any other party (buyer, seller, co-broker)

Loyalty conflicts with most other commitments. If the client's instructions seem foolish, the agent's duty is to advise, then follow.

## Disclosure

The agent must disclose:

- All material facts known about the property (to the buyer)
- All material facts about the buyer (to the seller, with consent)
- All material facts that would affect the client's decision
- Conflicts of interest (e.g., agent owns the property, agent's relative is the buyer)

**Material fact**: anything that would influence a reasonable buyer or seller's decision. Includes:

- Property defects (known, knowable, or disclosed)
- Pending repairs
- Foundation issues
- Lead paint, asbestos, mold
- HOA disputes
- Pending litigation against the seller affecting the property
- Recent / pending zoning changes
- Crime statistics or sex offender registry (if a buyer asks, must disclose)

## Confidentiality

The agent must NOT disclose to the other side (or anyone uninvolved):

- Client's price flexibility ("they'd accept $350k" should never be told to the other side)
- Client's motivation (divorce, relocation, financial pressure)
- Client's other offers or interest
- Personal information the client shared

Even after the transaction ends, some information remains confidential.

## Accounting

The agent must:

- Properly handle client funds (trust account rules)
- Provide receipts for deposits
- Render account of money received and disbursed
- Return funds promptly when due

## Reasonable Care

The agent must:

- Use professional skill and diligence
- Investigate facts within their expertise
- Avoid negligent acts
- Refer the client to specialists when needed (lawyer, inspector, accountant)

The agent is not expected to know what a lawyer or surveyor knows. But the agent is expected to recognize when those experts are needed and refer.

## Application across the transaction

| Phase | Most-relevant duties |
|---|---|
| Listing | Disclosure (defects), Reasonable Care (pricing), Obedience |
| Negotiation | Loyalty, Confidentiality, Reasonable Care |
| Inspection / contingencies | Disclosure (findings), Reasonable Care |
| Closing | Accounting, Disclosure |

## Common fiduciary duty errors

- Pressuring a client to accept a lower offer for faster commission
- Disclosing client's price flexibility to the other side
- Withholding known property defects
- Failing to investigate basic facts (square footage, lot size)
- Not advising the client to consult a real estate attorney for complex deals
- Mixing or misappropriating earnest money
