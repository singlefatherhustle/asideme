# Post-License Course Requirement

## What it is

Within the **first 12 months** after receiving your salesperson license, you must complete **25 hours** of post-license education.

## Why this requirement exists

The first year of practice is when newly-licensed agents most need additional training:

- Real-world experience hasn't yet caught up to classroom theory
- Mistakes early in a career can cost you dearly
- License law changes constantly — post-license course catches you up

## Approved post-license courses

The post-license course (25 hours) covers:

| Hours | Topic |
|---|---|
| 8 | Practical agency and disclosure |
| 6 | Trust account practice |
| 5 | Contract drafting and negotiation |
| 3 | Fair housing practical application |
| 3 | Continuing professional development |

Approved providers are the same set as pre-license providers.

## When to take it

```
License issued: e.g., January 1
Deadline: December 31 of same year (12 months)
   ↓
   Complete 25 hours of approved post-license course
   ↓
   Submit completion certificate to GREC
   ↓
   License remains active
```

## Consequences of missing the deadline

If you don't complete post-license within 12 months:

- License is automatically **suspended**
- Cannot practice real estate
- Cannot earn commissions
- Must complete the post-license course before license is reinstated
- May owe reinstatement fees

## Cost

Post-license courses typically cost $200-500. Some online providers offer them bundled with pre-license for discount.

## How it fits in the licensing cycle

```
Year 1 (license issued):
   Complete pre-license course (75 hr)
   Take exam, pass
   Get sponsoring broker
   ★ Complete 25 hr post-license course within 12 months
   
Year 2-3 (license renewal #1):
   Renew license with 4-year initial term
   No CE required at this point
   
Year 4 (after first 4-year term):
   First biennial renewal cycle begins
   9 hours CE required per 2-year cycle
```

## Common post-license errors

- Procrastinating until month 11 of the 12-month window
- Enrolling in non-approved course
- Choosing a course that doesn't cover all required topic areas
- Not submitting the completion certificate to GREC
- Letting the license auto-suspend by missing the deadline

## Tip

Schedule post-license course right after you start earning commission. Don't wait until you're "settled in" — the requirement is unforgiving and missing it suspends your income source.

Many new agents budget for this:

- Initial license: $125
- Sponsoring broker setup: varies
- E&O insurance: $300-500
- **Post-license course: $300-500** ← budget for this

Treat it as a known first-year expense, not an optional choice.
