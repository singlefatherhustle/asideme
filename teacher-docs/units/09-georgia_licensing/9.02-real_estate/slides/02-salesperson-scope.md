# Salesperson Scope and Authority

## What a salesperson actually does

A licensed Georgia real estate salesperson performs the day-to-day work of buying and selling:

- Lists properties (always in the brokerage's name)
- Shows properties to buyers
- Prepares offers and counteroffers
- Negotiates terms with the other side
- Coordinates with title companies, lenders, inspectors
- Manages the transaction through closing
- Receives commission via the sponsoring broker

## The hard rule

**A salesperson can never act independently.** All real estate activities must flow through:

- The licensed broker who sponsors them
- A licensed brokerage firm

Every contract, every advertisement, every earnest money receipt is the broker's responsibility — even if the salesperson did all the work.

## Listing agreements

When a salesperson lists a property:

```
Listing brokerage: [Brokerage Name]   ← always the brokerage
Listing agent:    [Salesperson Name]  ← can be named, but secondary
Sponsoring broker: [Broker Name]
```

The contract is between the seller and the BROKERAGE. The salesperson is the brokerage's agent of execution, not the principal party.

## Commission flow

Commission is paid:

```
Closing → Pays the brokerage → Brokerage pays the salesperson per their split
```

A salesperson cannot:

- Be paid directly by the seller or buyer
- Charge a fee directly to a client
- Receive any consideration except through the broker

This is the **#1 violation that leads to license suspension**. Even small "thank you" payments outside the broker are commingling.

## Advertising

Salesperson advertising must:

- Include the brokerage name as the predominant identifier
- Identify the salesperson by their license type
- Comply with Georgia license law disclosure rules

Common acceptable format:

```
JOHN SMITH, AGENT
ABC REALTY (770-555-1234)  ← brokerage prominently shown
Listed by ABC Realty
```

NOT acceptable:

```
JOHN SMITH REAL ESTATE   ← suggests independent operation
770-555-1234              ← no brokerage indicated
```

## What requires broker involvement

The salesperson cannot independently:

- Sign listing or buyer agreements
- Open a trust account
- Disburse earnest money
- Negotiate the brokerage's commission with another firm
- Approve a transaction that conflicts with broker policy

## License history transitions

Salesperson licenses are issued for **two-year cycles**:

- Initial license valid 4 years (initial term)
- Renewed every 2 years thereafter
- CE required: 9 hours per renewal (specific topics)
- License lapses if not renewed within deadline

## Common scope violations

- Negotiating directly with the other side's broker about commission splits (broker's prerogative)
- Signing a contract in personal name vs brokerage name
- Maintaining a personal trust account
- Operating as if a broker (suggesting they can supervise others)
- Advertising in a manner that obscures the brokerage connection
