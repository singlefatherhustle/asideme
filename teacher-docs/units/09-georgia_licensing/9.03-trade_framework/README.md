# Georgia Trade Licensing Framework

**Unit:** Georgia Licensing
**Topic:** Trade Framework (overview across all trades)

---

## The Legal Map to Memorize First

Georgia trade licensing is split between **two legal regimes**:

| Regime | Trades covered | Statute | Board rules |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Trade contractors** | Electrical, plumbing, conditioned air | O.C.G.A. Title 43, Chapter 14 | Department 121 |
| **General/residential contractors** | Residential, general | O.C.G.A. Title 43, Chapter 41 | Department 553 |

The statutory purpose language for both regimes emphasizes **public safety and public interest**, which is why exam questions repeatedly ask about competence, supervision, and licensed scope of work.

## Codes — Current vs. Tested

A point that matters both on the exam and on jobsites:

- **Statewide construction codes** (effective Jan 1, 2026): **2024 ICC plumbing/mechanical/fuel gas** + **2023 NEC**.
- **Publicly posted 2025 exam reference lists** still point to **2018 Georgia codes** and the **2020 NEC** for some exams.

If you are preparing in 2026, tab the current DCA code families for practice and inspection reality — but **verify which edition PSI will actually test from** before exam day.

## License Classes — Requirements and Exam Paths

| License | Scope snapshot | Minimum requirements | Exam path |
|---|---|---|---|
| Electrical Contractor Class I | Single-phase installations not exceeding 200A at service drop/lateral | Age 21+, application, 3 references (≥1 licensed electrical contractor), 4 years primary experience with limited secondary/education credit, **70** minimum exam score | Georgia electrical exam |
| Electrical Contractor Class II | **Unrestricted** | Same baseline; unrestricted scope | Georgia electrical exam |
| Journeyman Plumber | Statewide journeyman plumbing | Application, exam, **1 year** plumbing experience; journeyman exam is **open-book** per reference list | Georgia journeyman exam |
| Master Plumber Class I | Single-family, one-level dwellings for up to 2 families, and commercial up to **10,000 sq ft** | Application, exam, **5 years** plumbing experience with at least **2 as licensed journeyman** | Georgia master exam |
| Master Plumber Class II | **Unrestricted** | Same as Class I plus commercial/industrial experience | Georgia master exam |
| Conditioned Air Class I | Up to **175,000 BTU heating** and **60,000 BTU cooling** per complete system/job | Application, exam, **4 years** experience, **EPA cert**, board-approved heat-loss/gain and duct-design course, **3** qualified references | Georgia conditioned-air exam |
| Conditioned Air Class II | **Unrestricted** | Application, exam, **5 years** experience, larger-system experience, EPA cert, extra course requirements | Georgia conditioned-air exam |
| Residential Basic Contractor | Residential scope | Financial responsibility totaling **$25,000**, **$300,000** general liability, exam | Residential Basic + Georgia Business & Law |
| Residential Light Commercial | Residential-light commercial | **$25,000** financial responsibility, **$500,000** general liability, exam | Residential Light Commercial + Georgia Business & Law |
| General Contractor | Commercial general contracting | **$150,000** minimum net worth, **$500,000** general liability, exam, project/experience requirements | NASCLA trade exam + Georgia Business & Law |
| General Contractor Limited Tier | Limited commercial general contracting | **$25,000** minimum net worth, **$500,000** general liability, **$1,000,000** contract cap | NASCLA trade exam + Georgia Business & Law |

## Licensing Workflow and Failure Rules

### For electrical, plumbing, and conditioned air

Standard logic:
1. Submit board application and supporting documents
2. Get board approval
3. Register with PSI
4. Pass the appropriate exam with at least **70**
5. Finish licensure steps

**If you fail the exam twice**, Georgia requires a **board-approved review course** before a third attempt. The current board FAQs repeat that rule for electrical, conditioned air, and plumbing.

### For residential and general contractors

PSI states that candidates must pass **both** the trade exam and the Georgia Business & Law exam within the eligibility period. If a candidate fails **either** exam twice, Georgia law requires waiting **at least one year** after the last exam before taking a third or later attempt.

## General Workflow — Any Trade

```
1. Choose license class
2. Read board rules and scope
3. Collect experience, references, insurance, and financial proof
4. Submit application to the Georgia board
5. Board approval to test
6. Register with PSI (or use NASCLA path where required)
7. Pass trade exam (≥ 70)
8. Pass Georgia Business & Law if required
9. Finish licensure issuance
10. Renew on cycle and complete CE if applicable
```

That is the safest high-level workflow across the trade boards and contractor boards. Topic-specific deep-dives live in the other 9.04 / 9.05 / 9.06 / 9.07 units.
