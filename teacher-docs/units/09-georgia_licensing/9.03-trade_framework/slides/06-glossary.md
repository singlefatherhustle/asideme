# Glossary — Trade Framework

## AHJ (Authority Having Jurisdiction)
The government entity (city, county, or state) responsible for enforcing building codes in a specific location.

## Apprentice
An unlicensed worker training in a trade under the supervision of a licensed practitioner. Different from a journeyman.

## Background Check
Criminal history review required for license applications. LiveScan fingerprinting is the standard method.

## Board Order
Formal action by a licensing board on a specific licensee (warning, fine, suspension, revocation).

## Building Permit
Authorization from the AHJ to perform construction work. Required for most non-cosmetic work.

## CE / CEU (Continuing Education Units)
Hours of approved education required for license renewal.

## Cease and Desist
Order to stop unauthorized work; first response to unlicensed practice typically.

## Certificate of Occupancy (CO)
Document issued by AHJ allowing legal occupancy of a building. Issued after final inspection.

## Civil Penalty
Monetary fine for licensing violations, separate from any criminal charges.

## Compliance Officer
Investigator within a licensing board. Reviews complaints and audits.

## Construction Industry Licensing Board
The Georgia agency overseeing residential and general contractor licensing. Falls under the Secretary of State.

## Disclosure
Mandatory communication of certain facts (license status, conflicts of interest, etc.) to clients or the public.

## Disciplinary Action
Formal action taken by a board against a licensee for violation of rules or statutes.

## Dual Licensure
Holding licenses in two trades simultaneously (e.g., HVAC + electrical). Each requires separate compliance.

## Endorsement
Authorization added to a license for a specific specialty within a trade.

## Exemption
Specific case where licensing rules don't apply. Narrow and well-defined.

## Final Order
The board's binding decision after disciplinary proceedings.

## GC (General Contractor)
Contractor with overall responsibility for a construction project.

## GREC (Georgia Real Estate Commission)
State board overseeing real estate licensing.

## Hearing
Formal proceeding where disciplinary action is determined. Licensee may present evidence.

## Inactive License
License that is current with the board but not authorized for active practice (e.g., licensee on extended leave).

## Investigator
Board staff who reviews complaints and gathers evidence.

## Journeyman
Worker qualified in a trade who works under a master's supervision. Cannot operate independently.

## License
Authorization to practice a trade. Issued after meeting all qualifications.

## License Class
Specific category within a trade (e.g., Class I vs Class II electrical).

## Licensee
A person or entity holding a license.

## LiveScan
Electronic fingerprinting system used for background checks.

## Master Plumber
Plumbing licensee qualified to operate a business and supervise journeymen.

## Probation
License status during which the licensee operates under specific restrictions (often due to past violation).

## Public Record
Information available to the general public, including license status and disciplinary history.

## Qualifying Agent
Individual designated as the responsible party for a contracting business.

## Reciprocity
Mutual recognition of licenses between jurisdictions.

## Reinstatement
Restoring an expired or suspended license to active status. May require additional CE and fees.

## Renewal
Periodic continuation of a license, requiring updated CE and fee payment.

## Revocation
Permanent termination of a license. Most severe disciplinary action.

## Sole Proprietorship
Business owned by one individual. The simplest legal structure.

## Sub (Subcontractor)
Contractor working under another contractor's prime contract.

## Surety Bond
Three-party agreement where surety guarantees licensee's performance.

## Suspension
Temporary withholding of license to practice, for specified period.

## Trade
A specific construction craft (electrical, plumbing, HVAC, general contracting).

## Tradesperson
Skilled worker in a specific trade.

## Unlicensed Practice
Performing licensed-required work without proper credentials. Subject to civil and possible criminal penalties.

## Warrants and Liens
Legal mechanisms for recovering payment for work. See specific lien law slide.

## Workers' Compensation
Insurance covering employee injuries on the job. Required in Georgia for businesses with 3+ employees.
